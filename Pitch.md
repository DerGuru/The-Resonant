**Urban fantasy where magic is a skill, not a gift**

Jason Fischer is a 27-year-old municipal records clerk who's made peace with being ordinary. He works in a basement archive, lives above a laundromat, and nurses a quiet crush on Lina, the sharp-tongued chef at his favorite restaurant. 

Magic exists in his world — harmonic inflection, the ability to reshape reality through resonance and intent — but it's not for people like him. His test at sixteen made that clear: minimal potential, no future in the field.

Then he finds a file he wasn't supposed to see.

Buried in the municipal archives is evidence of a ritual catastrophe involving Elyra Voss, once one of the best inflectionists alive. The official story is sanitized, the records suppressed, the details carefully scrubbed. But Jason recognizes the pattern: something escaped. Something powerful. Something intelligent.

And it's been looking for someone.

When strange resonances begin manifesting around him — harmonic patterns he shouldn't be able to perceive, much less shape — Jason realizes two things: 

- First, the test that deemed him "insufficient" measured the wrong kind of potential. 
- Second, he's no longer alone in his own mind.

Navigating conspiracy forums, underground researchers, and the secret he can't tell Lina, Jason discovers that magic in his world isn't about raw power — it's about precision. The institutions that control harmonic inflection suppress anything they can't regulate, anyone who doesn't fit their model. They've built a system where only the "qualified" deserve access to magic.

But what if qualification was never about potential? What if it was about control?

As Jason learns to perceive resonance fields invisible to trained mages and performs inflections that should take years to master, the authorities close in on evidence of unauthorized practice. He faces a choice: hide what he's becoming, or prove that magic doesn't belong to the elite—it belongs to anyone willing to understand it.

But understanding comes with a price. The thing teaching him magic was locked away for a reason—and now he's going to learn what.

**The Resonant** blends the underdog magic of *Alex Verus* with the systematic worldbuilding of *Foundryside* and the partnership ethics of *Becky Chambers* — perfect for readers who want urban fantasy that asks: what if the system itself is the problem?
