# RAE Communication Canon (Clip, Carrier & Whisper Mesh)

> **Purpose:** Consistent, playable rules for dialog via RAE – incl. Whisper Clip, room carriers, and the Whisper Mesh. Clear separation, scene‑ready, with a cost model.

---

## Core Idea
If RAE wants / needs to communicate with someone else, than Jason, RAE **sets things into resonance**. 
Voices/info emerge through **harmonic coupling** – ideally right at the listener (contact) or via an object, that ic capable of resonating. **Jason** is the **primary anchor**: stability and range draw on him.

---

## Modes (clearly separated)

### 1) Free‑field on Jason (no dedicated carrier)

- RAE couples to Jason’s field and uses micro‑resonators near him (skin/hair/fabric/frame/the air itself).
- **Range:**
  - **≤ 30 cm** around Jason’s head → clear speech, normal speed, low effort.
  - **up to \~2 m** → “whisper mode” (short sentences, slower cadence), higher effort/artifacts.
  - **> 2 m** → effectively unusable without switching to a carrier.
- **Privacy:** moderate (minor air leak possible).

### 2) Whisper Clip (bone conduction)

- Passive clip on ear cartilage/eyeglass temple **or** a molar button.
- **Contact beats distance:** clear, private reception **within the same room** stable up to \~10 m radius (current cap), from Jason’s position.
- **Privacy:** essentially no air leak; no telemetry.

### 3) Room Carrier (railing/table/cup …)

- RAE “speaks” via an object in the room; selective, semi‑public carrier.
- **Effort:** higher than clip; depends on damping/overtones.
- **Privacy:** lower – in very quiet rooms someone nearby may notice. Wave forming using interferences gets some privacy.

## Blocking & Interference (complete)

### Hard lockouts (RAE stops)

- **Sustained very pure melodies let RAE become quite.** 
  Complex, noisy music has enough entropy to mask RAEs influence on the world around her; single‑tone purity (whistle, tuning fork, single sine form, single string from guitar/violin/...) or absolute silence does not.  
  Chords add enough entropy that can mask small harmonic transfers, but unreliable in high‑risk scenes.
  It is not, that RAE is uanble to use harmonics in these situations, but it is more likely that RAE will be detected, when doing so and therefore avoids such things - until RAE und Jason are ready to face any- and everything someone may throw at them.
- **Pure notes** at/near the active carrier’s resonance (whistle, tuning fork, sine from phone, single string from guitar/violin/...) will prevent RAEs harmonics to work on the carrier..
- **Jason overload**: onset of headache or worse ("Frontal ache" or Red) → immediate stop.
- **Carrier swamped**: the physical carrier is already dominated by a **steady external drive** (industrial motor, train wheel scream, PA feedback).

### Soft interference (degrades quality; possible with workarounds)
  basically everything that will make listening harder will make it harder for RAE and/or uses more invest from Jason, but it will make it also harder for RAE to be deteceted

- **Broadband vibration/noise floor** (subway, heavy HVAC, server rooms),
  - workaround efficiancy depends on the number of source to add harmonics to 
- **Wind/rain on the clip** (impacts bone‑path coupling; hand shielding helps).
  - wind makes noise, water adds weight
- **Loose fit / soft damping** (beanies, thick scarves over the ear/temple).
  - dampening sounds without masking RAE, when she tries to overcome the dampening
  - makes wearing the whisper-clip uncomfortable
- **Motion while free‑fielding** (running, head turns) → phase jitter.
- **Large reverberant volumes** (cathedrals, hangars) → long tails smear timing.

### Adversarial jamming (what an opponent can do)

- **Pure sine sweep** across speech band (200–6,000 Hz) in the room.
- **Beat‑lock trap**: metronome + held tone (locks phase, kills transfers).
- **Antiresonant pack** on targets (small **salt‑water** pouch/sandbag on a clip or rail) → shifts/damps Q, breaking coupling.

### Material & geometry notes

- **High‑Q metals/ceramics** are great carriers *and* easy to jam with a pure tone.
- **Foams/fabrics/soft woods** damp nicely (good for stealth; bad for long‑range room carriers).
- **Continuous structures** (rails, ducts) extend range *and* propagate jamming.
- **Liquids** couple energy unpredictably; avoid wet clips unless shielded.

### Workarounds (order of operations)

1. **Switch carrier**: room → clip → molar; or move the contact point by **10–20 cm** to dodge a resonance.
2. **Touch grounded material** (wood/metal) to re‑anchor; brief **micro‑bursts between beats** (off‑rhythm windows).
3. **Hand shield** over clip; tighten fit.
4. **Drop to internal** (Jason↔RAE), keep it ultra‑short.
5. **Relocate** two steps to exit a standing‑wave node.

**Rule of thumb:** *If it hums steady, don’t talk steady.* Use brief packets off the rhythm or change the thing that’s vibrating.

## Whisper Mesh (Jason, Lina, Milo, whomever – triangular)

- RAE mixes/relays **voice timbres** among the three and projects them onto each listener’s **active carrier**.
- **Prerequisite:** each listener has an **active carrier** (clips preferred; room carriers possible with leak risk).
- **Range:** stable **up to \~10 m within the same room**; through walls only if there’s a continuous carrier (e.g., continuous railing).
- **Floor control (half‑duplex):** only **one** voice “live”, others **dimmed**. Tap on clip/carrier = request floor.
- **Identity:** real timbres + slight **RAE patina** (glassy attack). **Latency** \~40–80 ms.
- **Limit:** max **3 parallel streams** (triangular). Above that → artifacts (sibilant smear/compression pumping).
- **Handshake:** short phrase when joining (e.g., “low tide”), then open.
- **Feedback guard:** two projected voices onto the **same physical carrier** get auto‑decoupled/attenuated.

## Dialogue Protocol & Formatting (open vs. internal)

### When RAE speaks **openly**

**Trusted People** 
- Jason
- Milo, 
- Lina
- later (after building trust) Elyra

**Condition:** 
  1) Only **trusted people** are present with Jason (no outsiders within earshot) or the szene allows for workarounds (figleaf, wave forming)
  2) The Szene allows the use of Whisper-Clips to enable sneaky communication.

**How to present in text:** 
  Use **normal dialogue** for everyone. 
  RAE is heard **via a named carrier** (see callout rule below). Whisper-Clips are just a carrier for this rule.

**Carrier callout rule:** 
  Name the carrier **once** when RAE first speaks through that carrier (e.g., "RAE, through the whisper-clip"). 
  **Name it again only if RAE switches carriers.**

### When communication is **internal** (Jason ↔ RAE only)
Internal communication is ALWAYS possible - but not necessearyly preferred.
Internal communication is known only to Jason and RAE. Nobody else knows about it's content and therefore cannot react to it.
Internal communication ist not speaking or saying, so use only words, that don't explicitly need to be verbal. 
    Good examples: convey, note, signal, indicate, send, pushed, expressed, relay, observe, clarify, warn, ....
    Bad Examples: speak, say, shout, articulate, yell, whisper, mumble, chat, ...

If someone else than Jason must be possible to react on something RAE communicates, then it needs to be open communication, which is actually preferred over internal communication. However, there are some conditions, where only internal communication is possible or preferred, because internal communication takes the least toll. 

**Condition:** 
  1. Only Jason is present.
    If only Jason is present or it is only to him and him alone - no other must or needs to know, internal communication is actually preferred over open communication. 
  2. Non-Trusted People are present and there are sensitive content concerns that make privacy very essential.
- **How to present in text (formatting):**
  1. **Jason** lines in **bold** (Markdown `**...**`).
  2. **RAE** replies in **italics** (Markdown `*...*`).

### Whisper-clips
Whisper-clips are a carrier of first choice and are preffered over other carriers. 
With the use of whisper-clips, RAE can also speak hidden from others, if such are present. This si still open dialog.
External carriers are a fall back, if a whisper clip gets lost somehow. Fallbacks are not called out as long as they are not used.

### Tone guidance (Part 2 specific)

- When **Jason/RAE are with Lina and Milo** and **no threat is active**: lighter, playful beats are fine. Keep RAE concise, a touch wry.
- When **threat or surveillance** is implied: the tone snaps back to a darker, theatening one - with the occasional banter.

### Carrier hygiene & presence

- **Callout frequency:** once per scene; on switch, call it out again.
- **Default order of preference:** **Clip** → **room carrier** → **free‑field** (only if within free field radius).
- **Silence triggers:** sustained pure melody, onset of Jason’s headache, or environmental vibration that overwhelms the carrier.

### Whisper Mesh etiquette (floor & safety)

- **Floor control:** half‑duplex; tap gesture or explicit call (“Floor: Lina”).
- **Handshake phrase** on live join (e.g., “low tide”).
- **Auto‑safety:** upon **“Frontal ache”** or spike in room vibration, RAE **dims/kills** the loudest stream first.

## Privacy/Forensics

- **Clip/molar:** minimal traces; small high‑Q resonance; no radio/logs.
- **Room carriers:** recognizable signatures possible (large objects, complex overtones) → measurable in silent spaces.

## Cost & Load (tied to Jason)

- **Max radius with carrier (current): \~10 m** as a sphere with Jason in its center. Beyond that **headaches** threaten (will improve with training).
- **Cost curves:**
  - **Free‑field on Jason:** low (≤30 cm) → medium (≤2 m) → high (>2 m → rapid headache).
  - **Whisper Mesh (clips):** base load **+1 per active stream**; three streams = noticeable strain.
  - **Room carriers:** costlier than clips (damping/leak).
- **Symptom ladder:** pressure behind eyes → brow tension → **headache** → mild nausea/mishearing.
- **Hard stop:** at onset of headache **cut mesh immediately** or shrink radius (or “Red” looms).
- **Duration & recovery:**
  - Light (1 stream, whisper): **5–10 min** fine.
  - Medium (2 streams or 5–10 m): **2–4 min**.
  - High (3 streams **and** 8–10 m): **<60 s**, then cooldown.
  - **Cooldown:** 1–3 min quiet/carrier switch (hand on wood/metal helps) → symptoms drop one tier.
- **Progress (training arc):**
  - Weeks 1–2: 10 m, 2 streams stable, 3rd only brief.
  - Weeks 3–4: 12–15 m, less latency/artifacts, headache starts later.
  - Later: either more range **or** more parallel streams – **never both** without paying a price.

### Prop/Build (Whisper Clip)

- **Appearance:** **transparent** ear‑hook/temple‑hook clip that clamps **behind the ear** (think a little like an eyewear earhooks). Low‑profile, looks like minimalist hearing‑aid hardware.
- **Contact geometry:** a gentle **spreader** applies light pressure on two points: (1) the back side of the ear (concha/antihelix region) and (2) the **mastoid bone** just behind the ear. This gives reliable bone‑path coupling.
- **Core stack:** titanium micro‑spring arm → thin **ceramic/quartz disc** (passive resonator) housed in a clear polymer saddle; optional soft pads at contact points to prevent hot‑spots.
- **Adjustability:** slight bendable hook to fit ear shape; micro‑ratchet on the spreader for “snug / medium / loose.”
- **Fit check (on scene):** whisper "check"; if the user feels a faint **bone buzz** and no audible leak, fit is good. If not, nudge the mastoid contact 5–10 mm.
- **Operating note:** don’t wear under thick beanies/scarves (damping). If wet, blot dry; liquids alter coupling.
- **Visibility & cover:** transparent frame keeps optics very low; reads as eyewear support or assistive clip. Works equally on eyeglass temples.
- **No electronics:** purely passive. No radio, no logs.
