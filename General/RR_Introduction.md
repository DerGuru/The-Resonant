# **Introduction: TuesdayPhysics**

Hi everyone,

I'm a 47-year-old software developer from a small town near Berlin, Germany. I grew up in the countryside, met my wife during university, and after moving around together a few times, we settled here with our almost 5-year-old daughter.

I've always wanted to write a book, but I never managed to motivate myself - mostly because I never found the right story idea. I've loved **Writing Excuses** for years, actively listening, practicing with their prompts, and absorbing everything from their discussions. I know quite a few of the books here on RoyalRoad, though mostly as audiobooks through Audible.

Then AI came along and replaced the tedious typing barrier. Together with an idea, that was rattling in my head, it gave me the final push to try.

I'm a big fan of Brandon Sanderson's approach to magic systems - the idea that everything must have a cost. I also love David Brin's **The Practice Effect**, which changes exactly ONE thing about our world and then thinks through the consequences all the way to the end. Highly recommended if you haven't read it!

For my book, I wanted magic with physical rules. I didn't want LitRPG - I enjoy listening to it, but I didn't want to write it myself. And like Brin, I wanted to change just one basic thing.

So I asked myself: **What if everything is frequency, and matter is just a self-resonant form of different frequencies?**

That's how the concept of **harmonic resonance** was born — which feels less like magic and more like physics.

---

You can probably see where this is going — I ended up posting my first book here: ***The Resonant***.

I'm on the home stretch with only a few chapters left to write before the conclusion.

There are 14 chapters already posted, with more coming every weekend - Fridays and Saturdays at 16:00 Berlin time (CET/CEST). 

***CONTENT WARNING:***\
There is a romance subplot included. You have been warned.\
Also mild violence/language.

If that sounds up your alley, I’d be happy if you check it out — and I’d love any feedback.

**The Resonant** follows Jason Fischer, a completely normal guy who gets caught up in something extraordinary. 

I hope you enjoy reading **The Resonant** as much as I enjoyed - and was frustrated by - creating it.
