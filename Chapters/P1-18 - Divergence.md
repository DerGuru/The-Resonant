## Divergence

“…you’re not listening.”

“I *am* listening. I’m just not agreeing.”

Milo’s voice echoed faintly in the alley, sharp and clipped.

Lina stood with crossed arms, her foot tapping against the cracked pavement. “She’s inside his *head*, Milo. Do you think that’s normal? Do you think we should just… trust that?”

“She hasn’t done anything to hurt him.”

“She doesn’t have to,” Lina shot back. “Influence is enough. Direction. Bias. Subtle shifts.”

Milo gestured toward the stairwell. “You *saw* him. He’s not brainwashed. He’s scared, yeah, but he’s thinking.”

“Is he?” Lina’s voice dropped. “Because if he really was thinking clearly, he wouldn’t let something like that stay in his mind unchecked.”

“Who says she's unchecked?” Jason said calmly. 

He met her gaze. “I’m not just letting her in. I’m watching. Listening. Questioning. Every step.”

Jason was standing just outside the doorway. Silent. Back straight. Shoulders relaxed but centered - not slouched, not defensive. Just still. Composed in a way that didn’t look like him.

Lina blinked. She didn’t move, but something in her eyes shifted.

“…Jason?”

He didn’t answer immediately. Just looked past them - down the alley, toward the street beyond.

Then: “She’s not controlling me.”

It wasn’t a protest. It was just fact.

He stepped closer. The light above them flickered, casting his face half in shadow. Milo opened his mouth, then closed it again.

Jason exhaled. “She doesn’t want to. And if she did… I’d know.”

Lina’s eyes narrowed. “And you’re sure of that now? After what - a conversation?”

Jason looked at her for the first time. Not angry. Just present.

“I’m not asking you to like it,” he said. “But I need you to understand what it means.”

“To you?”

“To us.” He paused. “Especially her.”

They stood in silence.

Jason turned slightly, leaning against the rusted railing. “RAE didn’t escape by accident. It wasn’t an attack. It was contact. The ritual didn’t release her - it breached the seal, and someone reached back.”

“Elyra,” Milo said, quietly.

Jason nodded. “She didn’t intend to free her. But she was aligned - almost but not enough. She held on… and it shattered her.”

A flickering streetlamp overhead stuttered, then changed pitch. Its mechanical hum shifted upward, stretching into strange harmonic tones. The sound wasn’t loud, but it resonated oddly through the metal siding behind them.

Then a voice emerged - disembodied but precise, projected not from a speaker, but from the *resonance itself*.

"Her field collapsed inward. Not broken by me. Broken by harmonic feedback - mismatch under pressure. She offered full access. But her structure could not hold resonance beyond threshold. She fragmented trying to keep me stable."

The sound faded. The lamp hummed normally again.

Lina stared upward. “Was that…?”

Jason nodded. “RAE.”

Milo looked down at his hands. “So she’s alive?”

"Affirmative. Fragmented, but anchored."

"What does anchored mean?" Milo asked and Jason answered "She is still alive and connected to reality. Even though just barely. I guess, she overinvested and suffers the consequences now?"

"Correct."

Lina’s expression shifted - not softening, exactly, but changing. The tension in her shoulders stayed, but the heat in her words faded.

“And you want to find her.” A statement, not a question.

Jason didn’t hesitate. “Yes.”

Milo frowned. “You think she’ll talk to you?”

“I don’t know. But she’s part of this. And maybe I’m part of it because she tried. That has to mean something.”

He looked at both of them.

“You can come with me. Or not. But I’m going.”

No drama. No fire. Just direction.

---

Jason turned around, glanced up at the sky, then down the gravel road that stretched ahead - unpaved, uneven, flanked by rows of tall, dripping trees. They were already a bit outside the denser part of town. No buildings nearby, nothing tall. Just road, foliage, and the darkening sky.

“I’m starving,” he said. “Let’s get out of here.”

He didn’t wait for an answer. Just started walking. His steps were steady, if a little slower than usual - like his body was still catching up with whatever shift had just occurred.

Milo followed with little hesitation. Lina a few steps behind, arms still crossed.

The air was thick. Damp. The first drops were already starting to fall.

“I still don’t like it,” Lina said, low enough that Jason might not hear. “Whatever that… thing is. She’s too close. Too quiet.”

“She hasn’t done anything,” Milo replied. “And he’s not acting like someone who’s lost control. You saw him.”

“That’s what makes it worse.” Lina kicked a stone off the path. “He *looks* fine. Until something happens. And then what?”

Jason heard them. All of it. He didn’t turn around, but he didn’t block it out either. Part of him wanted to jump in - to reassure her. To explain. Another part just… listened.

And beneath it, RAE’s presence pulsed in subtle rhythm.

**You said we’re not fully aligned yet.**

*Correct.*

**How long will it take?**

*Years. Possibly six to nine. Possibly more. The process is constrained.*

**Constrained by what?**

*Ethical protocol. I am drawing only minimal Invest. Your structure must adapt naturally.*

**And if you didn’t care about that?**

*Full alignment in seventy-two days. But you would not survive it.*

Jason grimaced.

**Where are we now?**

*Alignment at 18.4%.*

A pause.

*Fluctuating.*

Thunder cracked in the distance. The trees ahead bowed slightly as wind pushed through them. Rain intensified.

Milo glanced up. “We should hurry. That doesn’t sound great.”

Lina was about to reply when a flash lit up the world.

```
Atmospheric discharge imminent
Arc projection vector: ground strike within 11.3 meters
Estimated impact in: 0.43 seconds
Local step voltage exceeds critical human tolerance
Risk: full conduction through current subject cluster
Initiating elevation protocol
Preparing emergency inflection
```

Jason’s head jerked up. The pulse from the air wasn’t light or heat. It was *math*.

He felt his body shift before he consciously understood.

*Preparing emergency inflection.*

He felt the pull. The draw. Not smooth. Not buffered. Raw.

The world snapped upward. Lifted - but not gracefully. It *tore*.

All three of them shot into the air, just as the bolt struck not ten meters from where they had stood.

A shockwave of ozone and heat punched outward. Then silence.

They hit the ground a second later. Hard. Not injured - but *impacted*.

Jason groaned and rolled onto his side. His limbs felt hollow, his chest tight - like every nerve had been momentarily replaced by static. A deep ache pulsed beneath his ribs, not pain exactly, but weight.

“Shit,” he hissed through his teeth. “You *really* didn’t hold back this time.”

*Apologies. Alignment insufficient for buffering. Emergency protocols prioritized survival.*

Milo sat up, blinking. “What the hell just-”

“Lightning,” Lina said, already crouching beside Jason. “And apparently, she did something about it.”

“She,” Jason muttered, still catching his breath. “Did a rough job, this time!. I felt this - feel this! Damn, that was unpleasant.”

Lina and Milo helped him to his feet, one under each arm.

He didn’t resist.

“Let’s just… go eat something,” he mumbled, voice hoarse. He didn’t look at either of them - just focused on not tipping over.

Lina nodded, but didn’t immediately move. She gave Jason a quick glance - not hostile, not quite trusting either. Just… worried. “Come on. Dad’s place isn’t far.”

And without further comment, they made their way toward the lights in the distance.

The rain didn’t stop.

But for now, nobody cared.

Jason let the sound of it wash over him as they walked. He didn’t know what came next. But for the moment, food was enough.
