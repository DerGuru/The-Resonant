## Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 03 - RAE comes with the package

Date nights were rare.

Between training Kira, coordinating with Malvek's organization, and maintaining our own practice, we and Lina had maybe one evening a week that belonged just to the two of us.

Tonight was one of those evenings.

We'd chosen something simple: dinner at a quiet restaurant neither of us had been to before. No resonance discussions. No student progress reports. Just... being together.

"Your father asked about you," Lina said over appetizers. "Wanted to know if you're being safe."

"What did you tell him?"

"That you're as safe as anyone working with resonance can be. Which worried him, but he accepted it." She smiled. "He also asked when we're getting married."

We nearly choked on our wine. "He what?"

"Relax. I told him we're taking things slow. That we're figuring it out as we go." She reached across the table, took our hand. "But I've been thinking about it. What this is. What we are."

"And?"

"And I'm happy. Really happy. For the first time in years." She squeezed our hand. "You make me better. Challenge me. Support me. And I think I do the same for you."

"You do. Absolutely."

"So... where do you see this going? Long term?"

We considered that. "Honestly? We see us together. Building this program. Teaching people. Maybe someday having a life that's more than just work. Kids, even. If that's something you want."

"It is. Someday. Not yet, but someday." Lina paused. "But there's something we need to talk about. The integration."

Our consciousness tightened slightly. "What about it?"

"I need to know: When we're together - really together, intimately - are you... always aware? Both parts of you? Is there privacy?"

*This is important,* we thought internally. *Answer honestly.*

"We can be," we said carefully. "But we're not. We give you—give us—privacy. Complete privacy. Part of our consciousness... steps back. Goes quiet. Lets those moments be just ours. Just you and the Jason-part of us."

"Really?"

"Really." We looked at Lina seriously. "We understand boundaries. Respect them. We know there are parts of life that should be just ours—yours and Jason's. And we honor that."

Lina was quiet for a moment. Then: "Can I ask the RAE-part of you directly?"

We nodded. Shifted our focus slightly, let the RAE-aspect of our consciousness come forward. A nearby water glass hummed softly—old habit.

"Lina Morandi. We give you and Jason complete privacy during intimate moments. We are not always fully present. Not always observing. Some experiences should be yours alone. We respect that."

"Thank you," Lina said. "I needed to hear that. Not because I don't trust you, but because I needed to understand the boundaries."

"Understood. And appreciated." The glass fell silent as we let our consciousness integrate fully again.

Lina looked at us. "Okay. I'm good. Really good."

---

Later, back at our apartment, we stood on the balcony under stars.

"I have a question," Lina said. "Completely hypothetical. If we had kids someday... would the integration affect them? Would they be born... different?"

"We don't know," we admitted. "The integration is with Jason's consciousness, but genetics are complex. It's possible they'd have higher natural permeability. Or maybe nothing. We honestly don't know."

"Would that scare you? Having kids who might be like you?"

"A little. But also... no? Because we'd know how to help them. How to teach them safely. And they'd have Elyra. And the whole program we're building." We turned to face her. "Would it scare you?"

"Maybe. But I'd rather have kids with you - whatever that means - than not have kids at all."

We kissed. Slowly. Deeply. A promise of future possibilities.

When we pulled apart, we said, "We love you. Completely. And we want whatever future we can build together."

"Me too," Lina whispered. "Me too."

---

Inside, later, in the quiet darkness, we made love with the certainty of two people who'd chosen each other deliberately. Not perfect. Not without complications.

But real. And right. And theirs.

And the RAE-aspects of our consciousness, true to our word, receded. Not gone—we were always one consciousness now—but not actively observing. Giving the Jason-part of us privacy. Giving them space.

Because some moments belonged only to the two of them.

To him and her.

And that respect, more than anything, made us certain:

This could work.

All of it.

The teaching. The relationship. The future.

One careful choice at a time.
