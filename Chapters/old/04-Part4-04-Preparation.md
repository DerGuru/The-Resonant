# Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 04 - Preparation

The next two weeks blurred together in a controlled chaos of preparation.

Malvek's organization delivered equipment - resonance monitoring arrays, emergency stabilization fields, medical-grade pattern analyzers. Milo spent three days integrating everything into the warehouse's existing systems, building a monitoring network that could track both our patterns and Elyra's simultaneously with unprecedented precision.

"This is excessive," Elyra said, watching him calibrate yet another sensor array.

"This is thorough," Milo corrected without looking up. "If something unexpected happens during healing attempts, we'll know immediately. Down to the millisecond. That's not excessive. That's sensible."

She didn't argue. None of us did. Everyone understood what was at stake.

Meanwhile, we dove deeper into healing theory than we'd ever gone before. Not just understanding resonance restoration conceptually, but building muscle memory for the precise pattern manipulations required. RAE's analytical frameworks mapped thousands of potential approaches. Jason's intuition filtered them down to what felt right, what carried the highest probability of success without unnecessary risk.

Teaching continued, of course. We couldn't just abandon our students for two weeks. But we noticed ourselves distracted during sessions, part of our processing capability dedicated to healing preparation even while demonstrating field techniques.

"You're thinking about something else," Kira observed after one lesson. "Your demonstration was perfect, but you weren't fully present."

Perceptive. She'd grown a lot in five weeks.

"We have another project alongside teaching," we admitted. "Something important we're preparing for. But it doesn't affect our commitment to your education."

"Is it dangerous?" Sophie asked, overhearing.

"Potentially. But carefully planned. We're not taking unnecessary risks."

"Good," Marcus said. "We need you around to finish teaching us. Can't master resonance layering without proper instruction."

Their concern was genuine. Touching. They'd bonded with us over five weeks of intensive training, built trust in our capability and judgment. The thought of disappointing them by failing at healing attempts added another layer of pressure to everything else.

But also motivation. We were building something here. A teaching program that worked. A community of practitioners learning ethical resonance use. Students who trusted us to guide them properly.

All the more reason to approach Elyra's healing with extreme caution. We had responsibilities now. People depending on us. Couldn't take chances that might remove us from their lives.

---

Ten days into preparation, Malvek arrived in person for the first time since the RP-0 encounter.

He looked the same. Professional. Controlled. But we sensed something different in how he moved through the warehouse - less like visiting a monitored operation, more like inspecting something he'd invested personal interest in.

"The monitoring setup is impressive," he said, studying Milo's handiwork. "Medical-grade precision with real-time pattern analysis. This rivals equipment at our primary research facility."

"That's the idea," Milo said. "If we're attempting experimental healing, we do it with the best possible support infrastructure."

Malvek turned to us. "And you're confident you can maintain the precision necessary? Merged consciousness or not, this requires control at levels most practitioners never achieve."

"We've been practicing," we said. "Small-scale pattern manipulation. Building the fine motor control equivalent for resonance work. Watch."

We demonstrated, extending our awareness and creating a resonance pattern so precise it existed at barely perceptible intensity. Then layered a second pattern within millimeters of the first without any interference or harmonic bleed. Then a third. Then a fourth.

Four independent patterns occupying the same space, each existing at different frequencies, none disrupting the others despite proximity that should have caused instant collapse.

Malvek watched with professional interest that gradually shifted to something approaching wonder. "That's... extraordinary. Even specialized healers rarely achieve that level of precision."

"That's the advantage of merged consciousness," we explained, letting the patterns dissolve. "Jason's intuitive sense of what feels right combined with RAE's processing capability for maintaining exact parameters. We can hold stable patterns while simultaneously adjusting for instabilities before they manifest."

"Show me the healing approach," he said. "Not on Elyra. On something inert. I want to see your technique."

We'd practiced this. Created a resonance structure with deliberate imperfections, then demonstrated how we'd guide restoration. Not forcing change, but facilitating proper reformation through careful pattern alignment and energy distribution.

Malvek watched in silence, occasionally asking technical questions, probing our understanding of what we were doing and why.

After twenty minutes, he nodded. "You're ready. Or as ready as anyone could be for this. The theory is sound. Your technique is precise. If I were approving this officially - which I'm not, this remains experimental - I'd clear you to attempt."

Relief flooded through us. "That means a lot coming from you."

"Don't let it create overconfidence," he cautioned. "Theory and practice differ. Inert structures and living resonance patterns differ. Elyra's damage is fifteen years old, deeply integrated into her system. What works perfectly on practice targets might fail on actual tissue."

"We know. That's why we're starting small. Minor adjustments to test responsiveness before attempting major restoration."

"Good." He turned to Elyra, who'd been observing from her customary position near the room's edge. "And you're certain about this? You understand the risks?"

"Completely," she said. "I've reviewed their preparation work. Watched them practice. They're as ready as they're going to be. And I'm tired of waiting for healing that won't come on its own."

"Then I'll be here for the attempts," Malvek said. "Official observation. Medical backup if needed. And documentation - if this works, others deserve access to the methodology."

"Pressure," we muttered again.

"Opportunity," Malvek corrected. "To advance healing theory significantly. But that's secondary concern. Primary goal is Elyra's wellbeing. Everything else is bonus."

---

That evening, Lina came by our apartment. Ostensibly to review scheduling for next week's teaching sessions. Actually to check on how we were handling the pressure.

"You're worried," she said, settling onto our couch with tea we'd prepared.

"Of course we are. Two days until first attempt. What if we've missed something? What if our practice doesn't translate to actual healing?"

"Then you'll learn from it and adjust approach." She set down her tea, taking our hand. "But you won't have missed anything. You've been obsessive about preparation. Milo says you've run through protocols dozens of times. Elyra confirmed your technique is flawless on practice structures."

"Practice structures aren't the same as—"

"I know." She squeezed our hand. "But preparation matters. You've done everything possible to be ready. At some point, you have to trust that and take the step."

We were quiet for a moment, feeling her presence. Solid. Grounding. The relationship that had been awkward and uncertain five weeks ago now felt natural. Comfortable. Real.

"When did you become so calm about this?" we asked. "You were the one saying you didn't want to lose us."

"I still don't," she said seriously. "But I've watched you prepare. Seen how careful you're being. And I trust you - both parts of you - to not take unnecessary risks. That trust isn't blind faith. It's observation-based confidence."

She shifted closer, and we wrapped an arm around her. Easy intimacy. The kind that came from spending weeks learning each other properly.

"Besides," she added, "you promised me you'd be careful. And you keep your promises."

"We do."

"Then I believe you'll approach this healing work the same way you approach everything else - thoughtfully, methodically, with attention to what could go wrong and plans for addressing it."

"You make us sound responsible."

"You are responsible." She looked up at us. "That's part of what I love about who you've become. Jason had good instincts but sometimes acted impulsively. RAE had excellent analysis but lacked emotional context. Together, you balance each other. Think through implications while still maintaining human care for outcomes."

The words settled warmly in our consciousness. Recognition of what the merger had created - not just capabilities neither of us had alone, but a more complete way of engaging with challenges.

"Thank you," we said quietly. "For seeing that. For loving what we've become instead of mourning what we were."

"I'm not going to lie - there were moments I missed just-Jason." She smiled. "But what you are now? It's better. Not just more capable. More complete. Like you said about Elyra's healing - not walking with a limp anymore. Just walking properly."

We kissed her, and she responded with the easy certainty that had developed over the past few weeks. When we pulled back, we rested our forehead against hers.

"Stay tonight?" we asked. "We could use the company before tomorrow's final preparation."

"Of course." She settled more comfortably against us. "Where else would I be?"

"At your own apartment? Getting proper rest before your coordination duties tomorrow?"

"Rest is overrated." But she was smiling. "Besides, you're better company than my roommates. And you make excellent tea."

"Practical reasons then."

"Entirely practical." She kissed us again. "Nothing to do with enjoying your presence or feeling safer knowing you're not obsessing alone."

"Of course not."

We sat there in comfortable silence, tea cooling forgotten, just existing together. Tomorrow would bring final preparation. Day after, first healing attempt. Pressure and risk and potential for both success and failure.

But tonight, we had someone who loved us. Who trusted us. Who'd chosen to stay through uncertainty and transformation.

That was worth more than any healing we could accomplish.

Though we still planned to accomplish the healing too.

One careful step at a time.

---

The morning of the first attempt arrived with clear skies and nervous energy.

We'd slept poorly despite Lina's presence. Too many thoughts spiraling through merged consciousness. Too much awareness of what could go wrong. But also excitement - we were actually doing this. Attempting healing that could restore what Elyra had lost fifteen years ago.

The warehouse training hall had been reconfigured. Monitoring equipment surrounded a central space where Elyra would sit. Milo at his screens, ready to track every aspect of the attempt. Malvek positioned with emergency medical equipment. Lina nearby, supportive presence and backup if we needed assistance.

"Small adjustments only today," we reminded ourselves aloud. "Testing responsiveness. Building confidence. No heroics."

"No heroics," Elyra agreed. She sat in the designated chair, looking calmer than we felt. "Just careful exploration of what's possible."

"Monitoring systems are active," Milo confirmed. "I'm tracking both your patterns in real-time. Any instability registers immediately."

"Medical backup standing by," Malvek added. "Though I expect you won't need it for minor work."

Lina caught our eye, smiled reassuringly. We're here. You've got this.

We took a breath, centering ourselves. Jason's calm focus. RAE's analytical precision. Together.

"Beginning initial resonance contact," we said.

We extended our awareness toward Elyra, not forcing connection but offering it. Gentle. Careful. Waiting for her patterns to accept the contact.

She opened her resonance field in response, and we felt the familiar architecture of her damage. The fractures and blockages we'd studied for weeks. But experiencing them directly instead of through monitoring data was different. More visceral. More complex.

"Contact established," Milo confirmed. "Patterns stable. No adverse reactions."

We navigated carefully through Elyra's resonance structure, mapping in real-time what we'd only seen through sensors before. Finding the minor blockage we'd selected for first attempt - a secondary pathway disruption that caused discomfort but wasn't critical to function.

"Located target area," we said. "Beginning assessment."

We examined the blockage from multiple angles, understanding how it had formed, why it persisted, what prevented natural healing. RAE's analysis cataloged structural details while Jason's intuition sensed the emotional weight Elyra carried in this damage - fifteen years of accepting limitation.

"Ready to attempt adjustment," we said. "Elyra?"

"Proceed," she confirmed.

We began the healing work we'd practiced on inert structures dozens of times. Not forcing change, but gently guiding Elyra's own resonance patterns toward proper configuration. Facilitating what her system wanted to do but couldn't accomplish alone.

The blockage responded. Slowly. Hesitantly. But it responded.

We felt resistance dissolve. Pathways that had been constricted for fifteen years beginning to open. Energy flowing properly instead of routing around damage.

"It's working," Milo said, voice carrying surprise despite expecting success. "The blockage is actually clearing."

We maintained focus, not celebrating yet. Continued guiding the healing until the pathway fully restored. Then carefully withdrew our presence, letting Elyra's patterns stabilize on their own.

"Disengaging," we said.

Connection dissolved. We stepped back, suddenly aware of how much effort that had required despite being a minor adjustment.

"How do you feel?" Lina asked Elyra immediately.

Elyra sat quietly for a moment, sensing internal changes. Then her eyes opened, expression carrying wonder.

"Different," she said. "The constant ache in my left shoulder - it's gone. Completely gone. I hadn't even realized how much I'd adapted to it until it wasn't there anymore."

Relief flooded through us. It worked. Actually worked.

"Monitoring shows full pathway restoration," Milo confirmed. "No instabilities. No adverse effects. Clean healing."

Malvek studied his medical displays. "Remarkable. I've never seen resonance restoration at that precision level. You didn't just facilitate healing - you targeted specific structure and corrected it completely."

"That was a minor blockage," we said, trying to manage expectations. "Core damage will be significantly more complex."

"But possible," Elyra said, standing and moving her left arm experimentally. Full range of motion. No pain. "If you can clear minor blockages this cleanly, major restoration is achievable."

She turned to us, and we saw tears in her eyes. Not sadness. Hope.

"Thank you," she said quietly. "For caring enough to try. For being skilled enough to succeed."

"That was just first attempt," we said. "Small step. We've got more work to do."

"Then we keep going," she said firmly. "Carefully. Methodically. But we keep going until I'm actually healed instead of just functional."

We nodded, feeling the weight of responsibility and possibility in equal measure.

We'd done it. Proven healing was achievable.

Now we just had to do it again. And again. Building toward full restoration one careful step at a time.

Together.

Always together.
