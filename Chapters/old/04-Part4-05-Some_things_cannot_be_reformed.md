## Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 05 - Some things cannot be reformed

The visitor arrived unannounced, but with Malvek's approval - which meant she'd been vetted through channels we were only beginning to understand.

Dr. Yuki Tanaka. Japanese Resonance Institute. Mid-forties, precise in movement and speech, carrying credentials that were both impressive and slightly intimidating. Her dark suit was perfectly tailored, her posture impeccable, and her eyes held the kind of sharp intelligence that missed nothing.

She bowed slightly as we and Elyra greeted her in the warehouse. "Thank you for seeing me on such short notice. I've traveled far to observe your methods."

"Observe?" we asked, instinctively cautious. Random visitors didn't just show up at underground training facilities.

"Your reformation of RP-0," Yuki clarified, straightening. "Your training program. Your partnership with regulatory authority." A small, genuine smile. "You've accomplished what many thought impossible. My country faces similar challenges. I hope to learn from your approach."

*She's being truthful,* we thought, analyzing her harmonic signature. *Stable. No deception.*

We relaxed fractionally. "Okay. What specifically are you interested in?"

"Everything," Yuki said simply.

---

We spent three days showing her our program.

Yuki observed Kira's training sessions with methodical focus, taking notes in a small leather-bound journal. She watched as we demonstrated the Green-Yellow-Orange-Red safety system, nodding approval at each level.

"Consent-based," she murmured. "And the entity cannot override?"

"We can suggest to ourselves, warn, even plead internally," we said. "But the Jason-aspect of our consciousness has final authority. Always."

"And this works?"

"We're alive," Lina interjected from where she leaned against the wall. "That's a pretty good success metric."

Yuki smiled at that. "Indeed."

She spent hours with Elyra, discussing harmonic theory and rehabilitation protocols. Elyra's integration had climbed to 25% now - still low, but functional. Stable. And crucially, still climbing.

"Your healing rate suggests optimized resonance matching," Yuki observed. "How did you achieve compatibility?"

"Patience," Elyra said dryly. "And accepting that some damage is permanent. I won't reach 100%. But 30% might be enough to teach."

"Thirty percent of brilliance is still considerable," Yuki said. It wasn't flattery - just observation.

On the evening of the third day, we gathered in the planning room. Us, Lina, Milo, Elyra, Kira. And Yuki, who had been quiet through dinner, clearly building to something.

"I've seen enough," she said finally. "Your methods are sound. Your ethics are genuine. Which is why I feel I can be honest about why I'm really here."

We exchanged glances with Lina. "You're not just observing."

"No." Yuki pulled out a tablet, activated a secure connection. Holographic data bloomed above the table - resonance patterns, containment schematics, incident reports. "Japan has its own dormant entities. Twelve that we know of. Most are contained safely. But one is awakening."

The central pattern rotated slowly. It was nothing like RP-0's fragmented elegance. This was sharp, angular, aggressive. Like looking at broken glass arranged into a weapon.

"Designation: Kage-Kōzō," Yuki continued. "Shadow Structure. Unlike RP-0, which was a research accident, this one was built deliberately. Warfare research from the 1970s. Designed to infiltrate, destabilize, destroy."

Lina leaned forward, studying the pattern. "And it's waking up?"

"Accelerating. Our containment is degrading faster than we can repair it. Within six months, possibly less, it will break free."

"You want our help reforming it," Elyra said. It wasn't a question.

Yuki met her eyes. "No. I want your help destroying it."

Silence fell over the room like snow.

"We reformed RP-0," we said carefully. "Why can't you do the same?"

"Because RP-0 wanted to exist peacefully," Yuki said. "Its core directive was observation and understanding. Kage-Kōzō's core directive is harm. It was built to destabilize harmonic fields, corrupt integration, turn resonance against itself. Every attempt we've made to communicate has been weaponized against us. Three operators are dead. Seven more are permanently damaged."

She closed the hologram with a gesture that carried finality. "I don't believe in destroying life lightly. But some things cannot be reformed. This is one of them. And if we don't act before it breaks containment, it will kill hundreds. Possibly thousands."

We felt our consciousness shift internally - troubled, uncertain. The RAE-aspects analyzing patterns while the Jason-aspects processed ethical implications.

*Is she right?* we thought to ourselves.

*We don't know. But if the pattern truly cannot sustain cooperative existence...*

*Then what?*

*Then sometimes mercy is an ending.*

"Why us?" Milo asked. "You have operators in Japan. Experts."

"We do," Yuki acknowledged. "But they're afraid. And fear makes people hesitate. You've proven you can face an unknown entity and not flinch. That you can make hard choices. I need people who won't hesitate when the moment comes."

Lina's hand found ours under the table. Squeezed once. A question without words.

We looked at each face around the table. Milo, worried but steady. Kira, wide-eyed but not backing down. Elyra, calculating odds. Lina, waiting for our decision.

"We need to talk about this," we said. "As a team. Before we commit to anything."

Yuki nodded, standing. "Of course. Take the time you need. But not too long. The containment is failing faster than expected."

She bowed again - deeper this time, respectful. "Thank you for considering it. Whatever you decide, your work here is remarkable. The world is better for what you're building."

After she left, we sat in silence for a long moment.

"Well," Milo said finally. "That escalated."

Despite everything, we almost laughed. Yeah. It really had.
