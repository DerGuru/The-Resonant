# Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 11 - The day arrives

Sunday morning arrived with the kind of perfect weather that felt almost too convenient.

Clear skies. Gentle breeze. Temperature that promised comfortable evening. The kind of day planners hoped for but rarely got.

"The universe is being suspiciously cooperative," we observed over early breakfast.

Lina smiled. "Don't question it. Just accept that today is going to be perfect."

"Jinxing it by saying it out loud."

"Nope. Claiming it." She squeezed our hand. "Today we get married. Everything else is just details."

We'd decided against traditional separation - spending the night apart, getting ready separately. That felt like following convention for convention's sake. Instead, we shared breakfast, comfortable silence between bites, building toward the moment that would make everything official.

"Six hours," Lina said, checking the time. "Students arrive at five to help with final touches. Guests at five-thirty. Ceremony at six."

"That's soon."

"Very soon." She met our eyes. "Still ready?"

"Terrified. But yes. Ready."

"Good. Because I'm not letting you back out now." She was smiling, but we heard genuine emotion beneath. "We're doing this. Together."

"Always together."

---

By mid-afternoon, we arrived at the warehouse to find Milo already coordinating final preparations.

Fresh flowers adorned the ceremony space - simple arrangements that enhanced without overwhelming. Students from both cohorts moved efficiently, adding small touches that transformed the venue from prepared to perfect.

"Everything's on schedule," Milo reported. "Catering is set. Your father's in the kitchen making last-minute adjustments. Officiant confirmed arrival at five forty-five. Guests will start trickling in around five-thirty. You have—" he checked his watch "—two hours until showtime."

Showtime. Making it sound like performance instead of ceremony. But maybe that's what it was. Public declaration. Performed commitment. Real emotions displayed for witnesses.

"Where do we get ready?" Lina asked.

"Office space in back," Milo said. "I set up mirrors, seating, whatever you need. You're together until ceremony, then we'll coordinate entrance."

We'd discussed this. Traditional approach would have Lina entering after everyone was seated. But that felt too formal. Too conventional. We'd decided to enter together - visible commitment that we were partners already, ceremony just making it official.

The office space Milo had prepared was simple but functional. Mirrors. Good lighting. Privacy from the main hall where setup continued.

Lina had brought a dress - elegant but understated, deep green that matched her eyes. We'd chosen a suit - simple lines, comfortable fit, nothing that would make us self-conscious.

"Help me with this zipper?" she asked after changing.

We managed the zipper, hands steadier than expected despite nervous energy. She turned, and we were reminded again why we'd fallen for her. Not just beautiful - though she was. But genuine. Present. Real in ways that mattered more than appearance.

"You're staring," she said.

"Appreciating," we corrected. "There's a difference."

She smiled, then helped us with our tie. Her fingers worked efficiently, muscle memory from months of being together. When she finished, she stepped back to assess.

"Perfect," she said. "Simple but intentional. Very you."

"Us," we corrected gently.

"Right. Us. Still getting used to that pronoun applying to marriage context." She took our hand. "Ready to practice vows one more time? Before everyone arrives?"

We moved to the ceremony space. Still quiet. Students were finishing touches in reception area. We had these few minutes of privacy before the venue filled with guests.

Standing in the space we'd practiced yesterday, Lina took both our hands.

"Last rehearsal," she said. "Then it's real."

We spoke the words we'd practiced. She responded with hers. Different than yesterday - not scripted, just core truths expressed in slightly different language. That was good. Showed we weren't reciting memorized text but speaking from understanding.

"We're really doing this," she said after.

"We are."

"I love you. All of you. Whatever pronouns apply."

"We love you too. Have for months. Will for much longer."

She kissed us, and we stood there in the empty ceremony space, holding each other, building toward the moment when this private certainty became public declaration.

---

Five-thirty arrived with the first guests.

Mrs. Amari entered first, wearing a dress that suggested she'd been planning her outfit for weeks. She took a seat in the front row, nodded approvingly at the setup.

Malvek arrived with his plus-one - a woman we didn't recognize but who carried herself with similar professional competence. They settled into seats near the back, giving us privacy while maintaining oversight.

Kira's parents appeared, looking slightly overwhelmed but proud. Kira met them, introduced them to other students, helped them feel welcome. Her transformation over four months was visible - from uncertain teenager to confident young woman hosting her parents at her teachers' wedding.

Mr. Morandi emerged from the catering area long enough to take his seat, wiping his hands on a towel, unable to fully stop working even for his daughter's ceremony.

Students filled in remaining seats. Both cohorts present. Everyone who'd helped transform the space now witnessing what it had become.

Elyra and Milo sat together in the second row, looking satisfied with the setup they'd coordinated.

By five fifty-five, all thirty-seven people were seated. (Apparently two more had been added at last minute - couldn't turn away people who'd been part of the journey.)

The officiant - a woman Elyra had recommended, experienced with resonance practitioner ceremonies - positioned herself at the ceremony space center.

"Five minutes," Milo said quietly.

Lina squeezed our hand. "This is it."

"Nervous?"

"Terrified. You?"

"Same. But certain."

"Good." She took a breath. "Then let's get married."

We emerged from the office space together. No processional music. No formal entrance. Just two people walking to the ceremony space where everyone they cared about waited.

The ambient resonance harmonics Elyra had calibrated created subtle atmosphere - barely perceptible but present. Perfect.

We took our positions facing each other, officiant between us and assembled guests.

The ceremony was beginning.

Actually beginning.

Real.

---

The officiant spoke first, voice carrying clearly through the space.

"We gather today to witness the commitment of two people - well, one and a half, if we're being technical about merged consciousness - who have built something remarkable together."

Soft laughter from assembled guests. Good. This didn't have to be solemn. Could be joyful.

"Lina Morandi and Jason-RAE - we'll work on the naming convention later - you've asked to make public declaration of partnership that's already evident to everyone present. Marriage doesn't create your commitment. It announces it. Makes it official in eyes of community and law."

She turned to us. "You've prepared vows. Would you like to begin?"

We nodded, turning to face Lina fully.

Here it was. The moment we'd practiced. The words we'd found. Time to make them real.

We took a breath.

And began to speak.
