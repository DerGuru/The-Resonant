## Who Are You

The resonance lingered.

Not in the terminal. Not in the room. In Jason.

He could feel it behind his ribs, layered deep, like a note that refused to decay. Lina had stepped back, one hand still hovering above her Resonogram, eyes sharp, jaw tight. Milo was watching the space just beyond the console now, as if something invisible might shift into view if he blinked wrong.

Jason broke the silence first. "So what happens if we trigger it again?"

Milo tilted his head. "We don’t. Not yet. If it’s responding, we let it show us how. Otherwise, we risk destabilizing the structure."

Lina lowered her hand slightly, still on edge. "But it’s awake now. We can’t pretend we didn’t hear it."

Jason turned toward Milo fully. "Okay then. You said you’re not with Security. You said you used to be with Internal Field Stability. That’s all very vague. So who are you, really?"

Milo smiled faintly. "Took you long enough."

He moved to a support panel at the far wall, casually leaning against it. "I work freelance now. Liaison work. Sensitive diagnostics. Quiet fires. The kind no one admits ever burned."

"And you just happened to find us?" Lina asked.

"No," Milo said. "I flagged Jason three days ago. I just didn’t expect him to lead me here."

Jason's mouth went dry. "You flagged me? Why?"

"Because your signature changed."

That landed with a silence sharper than the one before.

Milo took another sip of his coffee, then looked at him directly. "Subharmonic bleed. Static harmonics that weren’t there before. Interference profiles that don’t match standard work exposure. I don’t know what happened to you, Jason, but it started before the archive."

Lina looked at Jason, quietly. Not doubting. Just re-evaluating.

"You think I triggered this," Jason said. It wasn’t a question.

Milo didn’t flinch. "I think it responded to you. Which isn’t the same thing. But it tells me there’s more going on than you’ve seen. Or remembered."

Jason’s fingers curled into his palms. He didn’t feel like a catalyst. He felt like a passenger. But maybe that was how it started.

"So what now?" Lina asked, voice low.

Milo’s smile faded. "Now we find out how deep this goes."

---

Jason felt the moment shift.

It wasn’t the room - it was him. Something settled beneath his skin like tension in reverse. No pressure, no pain. Just... arrangement. As if invisible threads he hadn’t known existed were suddenly being pulled into place.

He blinked. Lina and Milo were still talking - low tones, something about packet traces and null-gate harmonics - but their words sounded farther away than they should have.

A pulse vibrated in his spine. Faint. Then again, this time behind his sternum. Not random. Not alarming. Rhythmic.

"Do you feel that?" he asked quietly.

Milo turned toward him, frowning. "Feel what?"

Jason opened his mouth to explain - but what would he say? That something inside him had just... aligned?

He shook his head. "Nothing. Just static."

Lina was watching him now. Not with alarm. With calculation.

Jason looked down at his hands. The fine tremble in his fingers had stopped. Completely. And there was a clarity in his chest that hadn’t been there since - He didn’t know when.

*Who are you?*

The question formed in his mind uninvited, like a bubble surfacing from a depth he hadn’t known he held.

Only it wasn’t in **his** mind.

He had whispered it.

Out loud.

Both Milo and Lina turned to him.

"What?" Milo asked.

Jason didn’t answer. His breath had caught. Because the question hadn’t been his.

It had felt... returned.

As if he’d echoed something that had echoed first.
