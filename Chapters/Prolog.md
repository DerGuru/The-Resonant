# Prologue

## Break



The air was too still.

Not calm – not peaceful. But dead. Like the silence after a scream. Like the trembling of a spider’s web after the spider has been killed.


Elyra Voss knew something was wrong even before the ritual began.


She stood on the threshold of an ancient pattern etched into stone. Sixteen concentric circles stretched across the floor of the old observatory. The lines – fine runes,glyphes and sigils – flickered faintly in the twilight, as if even the light hesitated to interfere. The stones breathed history. And resonance. Too much tension in the field.


“Elyra,” the old master whispered behind her, “are you ready?”

She didn’t nod. Didn’t answer. Instead, she stepped into the center of the arrangement, where the heart of the ritual pulsed. Her boots touched the innermost rune, and a nearly inaudible hum vibrated through her bones.

It was a dangerous ritual – she knew that. A layered construct formed from multiple resonance domains: Lux, Umbra, Ferrum, Verd, and Aether. Too many. Far too many for one person. But she wasn’t alone.

Five other mages stood around the circle, each prepared to structure and invest. Body, mind, soul. Elyra was the focus – the loom through which all forces would flow.

The task was clear: an ancient fragment, discovered deep beneath an abandoned sanctum, was to be bound and defused. No one knew exactly what it was – only that it was unstable. And that it pulsed like a sleeping heart.\

“Begin,” she said softly, and they all moved as one.

The world changed in the blink of an eye.

Vibrations surged through her. Each resonance had its own color, its own feeling, its own taste in the realm of thought. Elyra felt the rustle of growth from Verd, the deep thrum of Ferrum, the crystalline clarity of Lux. Umbra was cold as whispered fog. Aether was barely graspable – only a shimmer at the edge of knowing.

The others came through. Hesitant at first, then powerful.\
She felt the offerings – trembling bodies, shaking emotions, opened souls.\
Her own muscles tightened. Her breathing grew shallow. Her heartbeat matched the pattern.

And the fragment began to awaken.

---

At first, it was only resistance. A pattern that refused to yield. Then it turned to rage.

Elyra felt the  resonance twist and lash back at her. Her eyes widened. The next moment, the outer rune cracked like glass under pressure.

“Hold it!” someone shouted, but she knew it was too late.

The formation snapped.

The pattern collapsed – not like a tent in a storm, but like a lung exploding. Vibrational force surged out, uncontrolled. It was as if raw awareness tore through her mind. The world lost color. Sounds turned to blades.

The mages outside the matrix screamed. One fell. Another bled from the ears.

Elyra remained standing. Forced herself to stay upright.

She would not release the pattern.

Not while the harmonic collapse kept surging through the structure. Not while it could cut through her.

She inhaled. And invested.

Not just emotion, not just will – but her very lifespan. Her focus narrowed to a point sharper than thought. The harmonics aligned. Stabilization returned. For a moment, everything was still.



And then— 

        —came the breaking.

---

She didn’t remember the fall. Only the moment the shape slipped away. When she fell into herself like into an empty mirror.

When she awoke, it was dark. No light. No feeling. Only silence.\

Later, they would say she had bound the fragment. That she had averted catastrophe. That three of the five mages were seriously injured, and one never spoke again.\

They would call her “the Last Weaver.”\

But Elyra knew only one thing: she had lost her connection to it.
