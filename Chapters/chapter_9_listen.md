## Chapter 9 – Listen

"Listen to what?"

Jason’s eyes snapped open.

"Who are you?" Lina asked sharply. "And how did you get in here?"

Milo Greaves stood in the doorway, one hand resting against the frame, the other holding a takeout cup. He stepped into the room as if it were the most natural thing in the world. His expression was half amusement, half concern - as if he wasn’t sure whether this counted as a prank or an emergency.

"Milo Greaves," he said, with the tone of someone announcing the weather. "And as for getting in ... well let's say that doors have the tendency to open for me. Sometimes literally."

Lina’s head turned slowly. Her eyes narrowed. "How long have you been standing there?"

"Long enough to hear you whisper something ominous," Milo said, stepping into the room. "And to realize this place smells like old power lines and guilt."

Jason blinked, still caught between the pull of the harmonic tension and the sudden weight of Milo’s presence. The resonance in his chest faded - but didn’t vanish.

"You followed us," Jason said, voice flat.

Milo grinned. "Sure. Or maybe I got curious when an off-duty staffer accessed a sealed wing under a deprecated authorization code."

Lina stood upright, posture guarded. "You shouldn't be here."

"I’m not officially anywhere," Milo replied, sipping his drink. "And you're not exactly running this through channels either."

"We didn’t trigger anything," Jason said quickly.

Milo looked between them. "Didn’t you?"

He walked toward the terminal, slow and deliberate. Not mocking - almost careful.

"You feel it too?" Jason asked.

Milo didn’t answer right away. He rested his fingertips on the edge of the terminal. Closed his eyes. Then opened them again, sharp.

"That’s new."

"What's new?"

"There’s something buried under the noise. It’s not active, but it’s aware. "

Jason met Lina’s eyes. She gave a tiny nod.

"Why are you really here?" Jason asked Milo.

Milo smiled, but the edge of it didn’t reach his eyes. "Because you’re not the only one who felt the shift. Something’s changed. And whatever it is... I think it started with you."

Jason said nothing. But somewhere inside him, the resonance stirred again - quiet, patient, waiting.

"Then let us listen," he said.

Milo raised his cup in mock salute. "I brought coffee. That counts, right?"

Lina didn’t laugh. And even though, she didn’t tell him to leave either, she kept a wary eye on him.

"Oh, don't give me the stink-eyes..." Milo said in a forced cheer.

The silence that followed wasn’t empty.

Something was listening back.
