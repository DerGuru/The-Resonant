# Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 10 - Transformation

Saturday morning arrived with clear skies and the organized chaos of twenty people transforming a training hall into a wedding venue.

Students arrived at nine sharp, energy and enthusiasm evident. Milo had printed detailed diagrams - seating arrangement, ceremony space, reception area. Everything mapped with his characteristic precision.

"Teams of three," he announced, distributing assignments. "First cohort handles seating setup. Second cohort manages decoration and lighting. Elyra, you're on resonance harmonics calibration. Lina, coordinate with your father on catering space. Everyone else, you're with me on general logistics."

The warehouse became a hive of purposeful activity.

Kira, Marcus, and Sophie arranged chairs in semicircular rows facing the designated ceremony space. Not perfectly symmetrical - slightly organic arrangement that felt more welcoming than rigid structure.

"Like this?" Kira asked, stepping back to assess.

"Perfect," Milo confirmed. "Creates intimacy without feeling cramped. People can see each other, not just the couple."

Second cohort students hung subtle lighting - soft resonance-infused fixtures that would create ambient glow without harsh brightness. They worked carefully, testing each placement, ensuring the effect enhanced rather than overwhelmed.

We moved between groups, helping where needed but mostly staying out of the way. This was their gift to us - their effort transforming the space. Interfering would diminish that.

"You're fidgeting," Lina observed, finding us trying to look useful near the catering setup.

"We're nervous," we admitted.

"About tomorrow?"

"About everything. The ceremony. The vows. Whether we'll remember how to speak in complete sentences when everyone's watching."

She took our hand, pulling us aside. "Want to practice? Right now, before setup is complete. Just us in this space that's halfway between training hall and wedding venue."

"Practice vows?"

"Practice saying what you want to say when it matters." She positioned us in the ceremony space, empty except for gradually forming seating around it. "Pretend it's tomorrow. Everyone's here. What do you want me to know?"

We took a breath, centering ourselves. The space felt different - not quite training hall anymore, not quite ceremony venue yet. Liminal. Appropriate for this moment.

"Lina Morandi," we began, and the words came easier than expected. "Five months ago, I didn't know if merged consciousness could work. If Jason and RAE could become something functional instead of fractured. You helped us figure that out. Not by fixing us, but by giving us space to figure ourselves out while staying present."

She listened, hand still in ours.

"You learned to love we instead of just Jason. That took courage. Took willingness to see what was actually there instead of mourning what had been. You built relationship with who we became, not who we'd been." We paused, finding the words. "Tomorrow we're making this official. But it's already permanent. Has been for weeks. The ceremony just announces to everyone what we've already built together. Partnership. Love. Future. All of it. With you. Always with you."

Silence for a moment. Then Lina spoke, her voice steady.

"Jason, RAE, we - whichever pronoun fits, you're still the person I fell for. The one who taught me that transformation doesn't mean loss. That becoming more doesn't require abandoning what was." She squeezed our hand. "You're patient when I'm impulsive. Analytical when I'm emotional. Careful when I want to rush. You balance me. And somehow, impossibly, I balance you too. Both parts. All parts. However we count."

She smiled. "Tomorrow I'm going to stand here and say something similar with everyone watching. And I'll be nervous and probably forget half of what I wanted to say. But this - this core truth - that's what matters. We built something real. We're making it permanent. And I'm grateful every day that you gave me time to figure out how to love all of you."

We stood there, hands linked, halfway between training hall and ceremony venue, practicing declarations we'd make tomorrow.

"Was that okay?" she asked. "For practice?"

"That was perfect," we said. "And now we're definitely going to cry tomorrow."

"Probably," she agreed. "But that's fine. Good, even. Shows it matters."

Around us, students continued setup. Chairs arranged. Lights hung. Space transforming. But for this moment, we just stood together, ready for tomorrow.

---

By afternoon, the transformation was nearly complete.

The training hall had become something else entirely. Seating arranged in welcoming semicircles. Soft resonance lighting creating ambient warmth. The ceremony space at center felt intimate despite the size of the room.

Mr. Morandi arrived with his restaurant staff, carrying enough food to feed twice our guest count. He surveyed the catering setup Lina had prepared, nodded approval, and began arranging his culinary feast with the care of an artist.

"This is more than we need," we told him.

"This is exactly what you deserve," he corrected. "My daughter's wedding. I'm not serving anything less than perfection."

Elyra completed resonance harmonic calibration, testing the ambient sound system. Soft, barely perceptible tones that would create atmosphere without overwhelming conversation. Elegant. Appropriate for practitioners.

"Everything's ready," Milo announced to the assembled group. "Tomorrow morning, we do final touches. Fresh flowers. Last-minute adjustments. But the major work is complete." He looked at us. "Your wedding venue awaits."

The students gathered, admiring their work. Pride evident in their expressions. They'd built this. Created something beautiful for people who'd taught them.

"Thank you," we said to all of them. "This is... extraordinary. More than we could have asked for."

"You gave us skills and confidence," Kira said. "This is us returning the favor. Community taking care of community."

"That's what family does," Marcus added, echoing Milo's earlier words.

Family. Built family. Chosen family. The theme that had run through everything for months.

"Tomorrow at five PM, everyone arrives," Milo instructed. "Ceremony at six. We'll have one hour between for final preparation and guest seating. Dress nicely but comfortably. This is celebration, not formal obligation."

As students departed, Lina and we walked through the transformed space. No longer training hall. Not quite wedding venue yet - missing the final touches. But clearly transitioning. Becoming something new while remaining fundamentally itself.

"Flexible space," Lina said, echoing our earlier observation. "Adapting to need."

"Like us," we agreed.

We stood at the ceremony space, imagining tomorrow. Everyone we cared about gathered. Vows spoken. Partnership made official. All of it happening in this room where we'd taught, healed, built community.

"Are you ready?" she asked.

"Nervous. Excited. Overwhelmed. But yes. Ready."

"Good." She kissed us. "Because tomorrow, in this space, in front of everyone who helped us get here, we're getting married."

"Yes," we said. "We are."

---

That evening, back at our apartment, we tried to relax.

Impossible, naturally. Tomorrow loomed too large. Too meaningful. Too imminent.

"We should sleep," Lina said around eleven. "Tomorrow's going to be long day."

"We should," we agreed. "We're not going to."

"No. Probably not." She settled beside us on the couch. "Want to stay up and be nervous together?"

"That sounds perfect."

We sat in comfortable silence, engaged couple on the eve of their wedding, too anxious to sleep but content to exist together.

"Thinking about vows?" she asked eventually.

"Thinking about everything. Vows. Ceremony. Everyone watching. The fact that tomorrow we'll be married." We paused. "Also thinking about Monday. When we go back to teaching and the warehouse is training hall again. Life continuing after this moment."

"That's good though," she said. "Means this isn't ending something. Just marking a transition. Life continues. We continue. Just officially partnered now."

"One more impossibility achieved," we said.

"Along with merged consciousness, impossible healing, revolutionary teaching programs, and learning to love someone who's technically one-and-a-half people."

"When you list it like that, our life sounds ridiculous."

"It is ridiculous." She smiled. "And extraordinary. And exactly what we built together. One careful step at a time."

We sat there as midnight approached, wedding day beginning in hours, comfortable in nervous anticipation.

Tomorrow would bring ceremony. Vows. Official commitment. Celebration with everyone we cared about.

But tonight we just existed together. Two people - well, one and a half - who'd found something rare and chosen to make it permanent.

Ready.

Nervous but ready.

Together.

Always together.

One more night.

Then forever.
