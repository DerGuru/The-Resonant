## Acknowledged

The restaurant had gone quiet around them. A few dishes clinked in the background, but most of the evening rush had passed. The storm still whispered outside, more drizzle than rain now.

Jasons body ached a bit less, but the fatigue clung to him like fog. Not just physical - something more woven in.

Lina shifted in her seat, elbows on the table now, watching Jason closely.

“I’ve been thinking,” she said.

Jason didn’t look up. “That sounds dangerous.”

She ignored it. “You could still walk away from her.”

Jason raised his head slowly. “We already talked about this.”

“No. We talked about cutting her out. Forcefully. I mean something else. Let it fade. Don’t align further. Let the connection dissolve on its own.”

Jason didn’t answer.

Lina sat down across from him. “I’m not saying she’s evil. I’m saying you’re not ready. This isn’t a partnership. It’s possession with polite manners.”

Jason opened his mouth, then closed it again.

Milo reached for a half-finished drink, swirling it absently before setting it back down.

He looked at both of them. “We’re doing this now?”

Lina gestured to Jason. “He’s still got a choice.”

Milo shrugged. “Do you?”

Lina shot him a glance. “You’re awfully quick to defend her for someone who met her about an hour ago.”

“Because I’m looking at results,” Milo said. “Not feelings.”

“You think she’s the solution?”

“I think she’s the only one even trying.”

He leaned forward, elbows on the table now. “And I want to find Elyra. Not just for answers. Because she’s the only one who had doubts - *before* the breach. I read some of her flagged notes in the oversight logs. She questioned the binding harmonics, the containment assumptions… stuff no one else touched. She asked questions no one wanted to hear. Then she went silent.”

He paused. “I think she knew what was coming. And I think she still pays the price.”

“So you think RAE will just lead us to her?”

“I think RAE *wants* to.”

He looked at Jason. “She deserves a chance.”

Jason rubbed his temples. “I don’t know.”

Lina leaned forward. “Then choose the safe path. Give yourself space.”

“Space for what?” Milo asked. “RAE saved all three of us, not 30 minutes back. That should mean something.”

Lina’s jaw clenched, but she said nothing.

Milo looked at Jason. “The alignment’s not done. You’re not locked in. If you start drifting, maybe she’ll let go. But right now? Maybe she deserves the benefit of the doubt.”

Jason stared into his cup.

“I don’t even know how I’d drift,” he said quietly. “It’s not like there’s a manual. I didn’t build this connection. I just... answered. But I don’t think she’s lying. And if she is… she had every chance to take control. She didn’t.”

Lina crossed her arms and leaned back.

“So we keep going?”

“For now,” Jason said. “We keep going.”

Lina nodded once. Slowly. Reluctantly. But she didn’t argue.

**RAE, I trust you. Maybe it’s just because our patterns align - I don’t know. And honestly? I don’t care. Just… don’t abuse it.**

*Acknowledged.*
