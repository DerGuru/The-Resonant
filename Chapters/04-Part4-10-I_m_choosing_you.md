## Part 4 - Boundaries & Becoming | Ch. 10 - I'm choosing you

*End of Week 6*

The HOA auditorium felt like a theater designed for judgment.

Fifty seats arranged in amphitheater style. Every one filled. Scientists. HOA officials. Approved media representatives with cameras and recording equipment. All here to watch us prove synthesis could be safe.

Or fail spectacularly trying.

We stood in the center of a marked circle on the floor—the test area. Lina beside us for phase three. Monitoring equipment surrounding us like electronic jury.

Malvek sat in the front row. Reeves beside him, tablet ready. Elyra in the third row, expression carefully neutral but eyes showing concern.

"Phase one," the announcer said through speakers. "Resonance control. Demonstrate precise capability without overinvestment."

We took a breath. Let our awareness extend carefully into the prepared test object—a crystalline matrix designed to show resonance investment visually. Too little and nothing happened. Too much and it shattered.

We invested. Precisely. Watching the crystal glow with exact amount of energy required. Holding it steady. No fluctuation. No surge. Green zone throughout.

The crystal pulsed with steady light. Perfect control.

Polite applause from the audience.

"Phase two: Stabilization. External object requires harmonic alignment without structural damage."

They brought out a damaged resonance carrier—intentionally fractured, unstable. Our job: stabilize it without forcing repair. Maintain structure without imposing pattern.

We extended our field. Felt the object's chaotic resonance. Found the natural harmonics it wanted to follow. Guided instead of commanded. Suggested instead of forced.

The carrier's resonance smoothed. Not fixed—still damaged—but stable. Contained. Safe.

More applause. A few impressed murmurs.

"Phase three: Stability variable. Associated person proximity test."

Lina stepped into the circle beside us. Took our hand. Smiled at us with expression that said *we've got this.*

The test was simple: maintain perfect control while Lina was in direct physical contact. Prove that intimacy didn't destabilize us. That human connection strengthened instead of weakened synthesis integrity.

We held Lina's hand. Maintained resonance field. Demonstrated precise control while someone we loved stood in potential danger zone.

Perfect stability. No fluctuation. Green zone throughout.

The audience's applause was more genuine now. Warmer. This wasn't just technical demonstration—this was proof that synthesis didn't require isolation. That we could be both powerful and safe. Both capable and controlled.

Malvek stood.

"Excellent work," he said, voice carrying across the auditorium. "What we've witnessed today represents a breakthrough. Synthesis previously thought unstable has demonstrated remarkable control. Unprecedented integration with maintained cognitive distinction. And most importantly—" he gestured to Lina and us "—proof that associated persons remain safe. That relationships continue. That humanity persists despite transformation."

He paused for effect.

"However." His voice hardened slightly. "One successful demonstration does not equal permanent stability. We require ongoing verification. Quarterly demonstrations. Continued monitoring. Regular assessment to ensure this stability persists."

There it was. The leash staying in place. The cage renamed but not opened.

"Quarterly demonstrations," we repeated carefully. "Not monthly?"

"Correct. You've earned reduced oversight. But oversight continues." He smiled slightly. "Consider it ongoing partnership rather than continuous surveillance."

The distinction was meaningless. But we understood the game. Accept the reduction. Appreciate the trust. Play the grateful subject.

"Thank you, Director," we said with exactly the right amount of gratitude and humility.

The audience filed out. Media representatives asked a few approved questions—all softballs, all designed to reinforce the narrative: synthesis can be safe, HOA oversight works, cooperation produces results.

We answered perfectly. Said exactly what they needed to hear.

And felt hollow doing it.

---

Outside, after everyone left, after the equipment was packed away, Lina and we walked through the city.

"You were perfect," she said.

"We performed well," we corrected. "That's not the same as being perfect."

"True." She squeezed our hand. "How do you feel?"

"Like a trained seal," we admitted. "Like we just proved we can jump through hoops on command. Like we exchanged genuine freedom for the appearance of trust."

"But you're safer now. They'll watch less. That matters."

"Does it?" we asked. "Or did we just make it official that they can display us whenever politically convenient? Made ourselves HOA's poster child for 'responsible synthesis oversight'?"

"Both," Lina said. "As usual. The situation is both better and worse simultaneously."

We walked in silence for several blocks. Then turned down a side street we'd never walked before—habit now, pattern-breaking as lifestyle.

"I need to show you something," we said.

"What?"

We led her to a small park. Empty at this hour. Benches arranged around a fountain that wasn't running. Moonlight filtering through bare trees.

We stopped at a bench. Gestured for her to sit.

"What's this about?" she asked, half-smiling.

We knelt. Not dramatically. Just... present. Real. Looking up at her face while moonlight painted everything silver.

From our pocket, we pulled a small box. Opened it.

Inside: a simple silver ring. Single small stone set flush with the band. Nothing ostentatious. Nothing that screamed wealth or status. Just... meaningful. Chosen. Ours.

"Lina," we said. "We're not Jason anymore. We're not RAE. We're not two people trying to coexist. We're... we. Whatever that means. However that works. This is who we are now."

She was crying already.

"We know this isn't simple," we continued. "We know loving us means loving something complicated. Something that will never be just one person again. Something that debates with itself and compromises with itself and sometimes doesn't know which part is speaking."

"I know," she whispered.

"But here's what we also know: We choose you. Every part of us. Every aspect. Every fragment of Jason and RAE and whatever synthesis created between them. We choose you. Not because it's optimal. Not because you complete us. But because loving you makes us more fully ourselves. Because you see all of us and stay anyway. Because you hold boundaries we need and give space we require and love us despite—maybe because of—our complexity."

We took a breath.

"So we're asking: Will you marry us? Will you choose this complicated, messy, synthetic 'we' for as long as we all exist? Will you write rules with us and break them carefully and build a life that makes sense even when nothing else does?"

Lina was laughing and crying simultaneously.

"Yes," she said. "Obviously yes. How could you even doubt—yes."

We slipped the ring on her finger. It fit perfectly. Of course it fit perfectly—we'd measured, optimized, calculated.

Then stopped calculating and just felt.

Lina pulled us up. Kissed us. Long and deep and real.

When she pulled back, she said: "I choose you. All of you. Jason. RAE. The 'we' you've become. With boundaries we wrote together. With love that's complicated and real and ours."

"We write the rules together," we said. "All three of us."

"Agreed," she replied. "Together."

---

We sat on the bench. Lina's hand in ours. Ring catching moonlight. City humming around us with life that didn't care about synthesis or surveillance or transformation.

"When?" she asked.

"Soon," we said. "We need to plan. Find location. Invite people. Make it real instead of rushed."

"Small though," she said. "Just people who matter."

"Just people who matter," we agreed. "Elyra. Milo. Mrs. Amari. Your father. Maybe a few others. No spectacle. No HOA presence. Just us and people who choose to witness."

"Malvek will want to be there," Lina said. "For security. For oversight."

"Then he can be invited as guest," we said firmly. "Not overseer. Not security. Guest. He can accept those terms or stay away."

"He won't like that."

"He doesn't have to like it. He just has to respect it." We squeezed her hand. "This is ours. Not HOA's. Not a demonstration. Not a performance. Ours."

Lina leaned her head on our shoulder.

"I love you," she said simply.

"We love you," we replied. "However that's counted. Whatever that means. We love you."

---

Our phone buzzed. Message from Reeves: *"Report immediately. RP-0 incident. Urgent."*

We looked at the screen. At Lina. At the ring on her finger.

"Duty calls," she said, not quite managing to hide her frustration.

"Yes." We stood, helping her up. "But tonight—tonight we got engaged. Tonight we chose each other despite everything. Tonight was ours."

"And tomorrow?" she asked.

"Tomorrow we deal with RP-0. With HOA. With whatever crisis can't wait." We started walking toward the facility. "But tonight stays ours. They can't take that. We don't let them."

She nodded. Held our hand tighter.

And together, we went to face whatever new crisis awaited.

But engaged. Chosen. Ours.

That couldn't be taken away.

---

*The demonstration was over.*

*The proposal was made.*

*The answer was yes.*

*Tomorrow would bring new tests.*

*But tonight: joy despite surveillance.*

*Love despite control.*

*Choice despite cage.*

*Sometimes that was the only victory that mattered.*
