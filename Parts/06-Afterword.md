# Afterword

Remember in the preface when I promised you transparency about the writing process? Here it is.

What you're about to read is the original AI-generated version of what eventually became "To be less" - back when it was still called "Anchor." This was supposed to be a codeword: Lina says "Anchor," and Jason/RAE shift to be more... well, Jason.

When I first read this draft, my immediate reaction was, that this felt too much like a therapy session. Five people sitting around, formal boundary negotiations, documented agreements - it was technically correct, but just ... off. Not what I wanted for this story.

So I stripped it down. Cut out the facilitators and formal structures. Removed the multiple "boundaries" and codewords. Instead, I focused on Jason and Lina figuring things out themselves - messy, intimate, real. Just two people (or three) trying to make it work. The result? That single, vulnerable request: "Can you be less?" Less perfect. Less optimized. More like the Jason she fell in love with.

I'm showing you this original version for two reasons:

First, because I promised transparency. This is what AI-assisted writing actually looks like. The AI gave me this, and I had to recognize what worked (the emotional core, Lina's fear) and what didn't (everything else).

Second, because I think it's fascinating to see how much the creative process matters. Same characters, same basic conflict, completely different emotional impact and outcome. I think, that the revised version works better *because* I cut away all the scaffolding the AI thought was necessary.

So go ahead - read this unmodified original. See what I mean. And maybe you'll appreciate "To be less" even more for what it became.
