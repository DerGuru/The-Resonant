## What Works Well

### ? Lina's Physical Description

**EXACTLY what the CHANGELOG asked for:**

> "Lina Morandi was twenty-four, though she carried herself with the confidence of someone older. Her dark hair - thick, straight, the kind of Asian hair that refused to do anything but what it wanted - was pulled back in a practical twist today, a few escaped strands framing her face."

**Perfect execution:**
- Natural integration into narrative
- Specific without clinical
- Cultural heritage mentioned organically
- Practical details (twist, paint-stained jeans, boots)

**Best detail:**
> "And if you looked close - which Jason tried very hard not to - a faint scar on the inside of her left wrist, about two centimeters long."

**This is brilliant characterization:**
1. Shows Jason's attraction through avoidance
2. Gives Lina history (kitchen accident)
3. Specific, memorable physical marker
4. "Which Jason tried very hard not to" - shows restraint/longing

### ? Jason's Unspoken Feelings

**The CHANGELOG specifically called for this - delivered perfectly:**

> "And then he'd think about things he had no right to think about. Things like: what would happen if he just said it?"

**Why this works:**
- Shows rather than tells
- Creates dramatic tension
- Makes Jason sympathetic (wants but won't take)
- Explains their dynamic

**Best moment:**
> "Because Lina was his friend. His *only* friend, really. And friends were rare enough that he wasn't about to risk it on something as stupid and selfish as feelings."

**This is the core of Jason's character:**
- Values what he has over what he wants
- Self-sacrificing but frames it as self-preservation
- Calls love "stupid and selfish" (self-deprecation)
- Fear of loss drives his choices

### ? Show Don't Tell Relationship

**The affection is shown through:**

**Small physical contact:**
> "Lina nudged his shoulder with hers, a casual gesture that sent a small jolt through him that he absolutely did not think about."

**Lina's scent memory:**
> "Close enough that he could smell the faint scent of kitchen - lemon cleaner, roasted garlic, something green and fresh. It was comforting in a way he couldn't quite explain."

**Shared humor:**
> "That one looks suspiciously un-depressing."
> "Or it could just be a toaster."

**Comfortable silence:**
> "For a few seconds, they stood in silence. Comfortable, but heavy with something else."

**No declarations needed - we feel their connection.**

### ? Setting and Atmosphere

**The flea market is vivid:**
- "More noise than purpose"
- "Fold-out tables, milk crates, and handwritten price tags"
- "Rusted tools and vintage magazines"
- "A man with a beard like insulation foam"

**The fence scene:**
- "Sagging chain-link fence and patches of weed-choked gravel"
- "A piece of torn plastic bag flutter against the fence"
- "Faint scent of engine oil and fried dough"

**These details ground the scene in reality and create mood.**

### ? Dialogue

**Natural, character-specific voices:**

**Lina:**
> "You have no principle. You want a toaster that doesn't smell like despair."

Dry, direct, funny. Matches her dossier ("dry humor").

**Jason:**
> "Three prongs. No visible scorch marks. Could be a trap."

Cautious, analytical, self-aware humor.

**Both:**
> "Just two people and a toaster," Lina agreed, her voice carrying that dry humor he'd come to recognize as affection. Or at least tolerance.

The "or at least tolerance" is pure Jason - can't quite believe someone cares about him.

### ? Lina's Perspective Moment

**The ending delivers on CHANGELOG goal:**

> "Lina paused at the next intersection, glancing back. But Jason had already turned the corner, and she was left standing in the rain, her expression unreadable. She pulled out her phone, typed a message, then deleted it without sending."

**Why this works:**
1. Shifts POV subtly to show Lina's side
2. "Expression unreadable" - keeps her feelings ambiguous
3. Typed and deleted message - shows hesitation mirrors Jason's
4. Makes this a two-sided tension, not one-sided pining

**Ending line:**
> "Some things, she'd learned, were better left unsaid. At least for now."

**Parallel to Jason's choice not to confess. Both are holding back.**
