### ? Quality vs. Firmness (System Building)

**Elyra's Explanation:**
```markdown
"Quality is harmonic alignment. How well your pattern fits the material's natural resonance. Steel has a clean, sharp signature - high quality for frequency-based work. Wood is more complex, with organic variations - good quality for pattern storage, but harder to control."

"Firmness is structural tolerance. How much the material can hold before it breaks down. This steel has high firmness. You can invest heavily, push aggressive patterns, and it'll hold. But this wooden block? Firmness is moderate. Push too hard and you'll fracture the grain structure."
```

**Why This System Works:**

**Two Distinct Properties:**
- **Quality:** How well it resonates
- **Firmness:** How much it can hold
- Not same thing (important)
- Both matter for different reasons

**Material Examples:**
- Steel: High quality + high firmness (frequency work)
- Wood: Good quality + moderate firmness (pattern storage)
- Glass: Between (versatile but less optimal)

**Practical Application:**
- Not abstract theory
- Specific use cases
- "Read before you invest"
- Prevents mistakes

**Life-or-Death Stakes:**
```markdown
"Learning to read that before you invest is what separates competent practitioners from dead ones. You don't test firmness by breaking things. You sense it. Respect it. Work within its limits."
```

**Perfect Because:**
- "Dead ones" = not hyperbole
- Respect material limits
- Precision prevents death
- Title theme reinforced

**System Elegance:**
- Simple enough to understand
- Complex enough to be interesting
- Memorable (Quality + Firmness)
- Applicable to many scenarios
