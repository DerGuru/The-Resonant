# Alpha Reader Feedback: P2-10 - "Destroy everything"

## Overall Impression
**Rating: 8/10**

Strong escalation chapter that raises stakes through institutional power display. The archive erasure is an effective threat that doesn't require violence—showing Malvek's reach and forcing a strategic pivot. Good pacing and character reactions.

## Strengths
- **Power demonstration**: Archive erasure shows Malvek's reach without confrontation
- **Strategic pivot**: Elyra's advice to "stop looking backward, start building forward" is wise
- **Paranoia payoff**: Milo's multiple backups feel earned from his character
- **Threat escalation**: From recruitment (P2-09) to active suppression
- **Character wisdom**: "Truth should matter" / "Power matters more" exchange is strong

## Minor Issues
- **Slightly abrupt**: The erasure and team meeting compress a lot of action
- **Emotional impact**: Could show more of Jason's frustration/fear at losing the evidence

## Suggestions
- Brief scene showing Jason trying different archives, realizing the scope of erasure
- More visceral reaction to losing months of research

## Bottom Line
Effective chapter that advances plot and raises stakes. The institutional threat feels real and forces characters to adapt strategy.

**Rating: 8/10**
