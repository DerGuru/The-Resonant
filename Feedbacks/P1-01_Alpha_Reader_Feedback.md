## What Needs Work

### ?? Pacing Issues

**The middle section drags slightly:**
- The break room scene (water at tap, reflection in window) feels like filler
- "Get a grip, Fischer" mantra appears but isn't quite earned yet
- Consider tightening the sequence from leaving desk ? returning to desk

**Suggestion:** Cut 2-3 paragraphs from the middle, or add a small discovery/interaction to justify the break.

### ?? Repetitive Language

**"Archive" appears very frequently:**
- "archival room" / "archival dive" / "archival layers" / "archive entry" / "archive code"
- Varies between "archive" and "archival" but still repetitive
- **Suggestion:** Replace 2-3 instances with "records system" / "database" / "digital vault"

**"Frowned" appears 3 times in ~2000 words:**
- "Jason frowned deeply"
- "He frowned, replaying conversations"
- "Jason frowned, took another sip"
- **Suggestion:** Replace one with "His brow furrowed" or "He grimaced"

### ?? Character Voice Consistency

**The "malicious look" moment feels out of character:**
> "He grinned to himself, tilting his head until two street lights aligned with his eyes, giving his reflection a malicious look."

This playful moment doesn't quite fit Jason's established interiority. He's self-conscious and resigned, not whimsical.

**Suggestion:** Replace with something more introspective:
> "He stared at his reflection until it became unfamiliar - just shapes and shadows. Twenty-seven years old and already feeling like a ghost."

### ?? Show vs. Tell

**One instance of telling where showing would be stronger:**
> "The question surfaced unbidden, and he pushed it away like he always did."

We're *told* he always does this, but we haven't seen it before.

**Suggestion:** Show the habit:
> "The question surfaced. He closed his eyes. Breathed. Counted to five. The old ritual. Push it down. Lock it away. Move on."

## Technical Issues

### Clarity

**The archive entry formatting:**
```
[Ritual Protocol: Anomaly Binding / South Central Node / Archive Code #E-VOSS]
```

This works well, but **"Focus: Voss, Elyra"** might confuse some readers. Is "Focus" a role? A person? A thing?

**Suggestion:** Add one line of Jason's internal thought:
> *Focus - the central mage in a ritual binding. The one who channels everything.*

### Timeline

**"Eleven years ago" is implied but not stated.**

From Prologue Ch. 01, we know the ritual was ~11 years before "present day." This chapter should be roughly concurrent with Prologue Ch. 03.

**Suggestion:** Add a subtle timestamp:

### Tech Consistency

**"Comms unit" appears once:**
> "A message awaited him on his comms unit"

This is the only time this term is used. Earlier it was just "terminal" and "screen."

**Suggestion:** Either:
1. Call it "phone" (more relatable)
2. Establish "comms unit" earlier as the standard term

## Character Consistency Check

### Jason's Test at Sixteen

**From Combined Dossiers:**
> "He's never tried. Not seriously. Even in his test with 16. Because what if he did… and failed?"

**But in this chapter:**
> "He'd been tested at sixteen, like everyone else. Minimal potential. Not worth training."

**Potential contradiction:** Did he try or not try during the test?

**Recommendation:** Clarify this. Either:
1. He went through the motions but didn't truly engage (fear of failure)
2. He tried genuinely and got a genuine "low potential" result

The internal conflict is stronger if he *didn't really try* because of fear. Consider adjusting:
> "He'd been tested at sixteen. Sat there with electrodes on his temples, *not quite trying*, and gotten exactly the result he'd been afraid to challenge. Minimal potential. Not worth training."
