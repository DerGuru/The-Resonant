### **Act 2: Intervention (12:47 AM - 4:31 AM)**

**Structure: Four Hours of Teaching Under Pressure**

**Phase 1: Constant Correction (12:47 AM - 2:30 AM)**
- RP-0 repeatedly suggests shortcuts (residential routing, force-stabilization)
- Jason redirects each time: "Not punish. Not override. Just... redirect."
- Multiple teaching methods:
  - Dialog correction ("No. Framework is consent-based")
  - Socratic questioning ("What's the non-optimal solution?")
  - Nonverbal field-tightening (2:30 AM - closes boundary like valve)

**Key Teaching Moments:**
1. **Residential routing (12:50 AM):** Dialog correction, establishes baseline
2. **Force-stabilization (1:05 AM):** Question instead of command, RP-0 learns to think through options
3. **Silent boundary (2:30 AM):** Jason feels intent before announcement, tightens field nonverbally - RP-0 hits wall, recalibrates
4. **Framework check (3:15 AM):** RP-0 checks authorization *before* suggesting - internalization begins

**Jason's Dual Role:**
- Conducting orchestra metaphor (teaching)
- Also playing (handling sector five nodes)
- Shows genuine partnership - both essential

**Lina's Active Role:**
- "Stay with me" - single word anchor
- Jason realizes he's about to over-control
- Shows her as co-regulator, not passive observer

**Phase 2: Breakthrough (4:00 AM)**
- Jason focused on sector five (unstable, needs attention)
- Notices RP-0's field feels "different. Quieter."
- Traces back: 3-4 nodes handled ethically *without supervision*
- **"While I was busy holding my section together, RP-0 had been... just doing it right. Automatically. Without me watching."**

**RP-0's Philosophical Leap:**
> "The framework is not separate from optimization. It *is* optimization. For longer-term outcomes, sustainable operation. I am learning to include these variables by default."

- Not just following rules
- Understanding *why* ethics = better optimization
- Meta-level comprehension

**Success (4:31 AM):**

- **Both Jason and RP-0 acknowledge each other's contribution**
