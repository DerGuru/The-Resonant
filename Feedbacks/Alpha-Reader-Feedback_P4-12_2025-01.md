## Structural Overview

### **Opening Frame: "If you're picturing a church..."**

**Purpose:** Establishes conversational tone immediately

**What it does:**
```markdown
If you're picturing a church, you're already wrong.

We got married in the same warehouse where I'd learned to hold 
boundaries with Lina without breaking each other. Where Elyra 
had been healed. There was bread on real plates, chairs that 
didn't match, and a few people who mattered.
```

? **Conversational:** "you're already wrong" = direct address, informal  
? **Sets expectations:** Not traditional wedding, warehouse = their space  
? **Grounding details:** bread, mismatched chairs, "few people"  
? **Kitchen-table feel:** Like telling story over wine

**Why this works:**
- Reader immediately feels like they're being told a story BY Jason
- Not observing wedding, but hearing about it from participant
- Tone established: warm, direct, personal, slightly irreverent

### **Act 1: Ceremony (~1,200 words)**

**Structure:**
1. Setting described (warehouse transformed but recognizable)
2. Guests cataloged (Malvek, Reeves, Milo, Mrs. Amari, parents)
3. Lina's vows (chooses "all of you" - Jason, RAE, synthesis)
4. Jason's vows (acknowledges complexity, chooses partnership)
5. Ring exchange (imperfection: almost dropped, both catch, laugh)
6. Kiss (all aspects present)

**Vows - Lina:**
```markdown
"I choose you knowing you're complicated. Knowing you'll never 
be simple again. Knowing that loving you means loving something 
most people won't fully understand. I choose you with all the 
difficulty that brings - the questions, the fear, the uncertainty. 
And I choose you anyway."
```

**Key:** "And I choose you anyway" = active choice despite difficulty, not ignorance of it

**Vows - Jason:**
```markdown
"I choose love that's practiced deliberately instead of assumed 
automatically. I choose commitment that costs something - presence, 
attention, boundary-holding, patience - and give it anyway."
```

**Key:** Love as practice, commitment as cost willingly paid

**Imperfection Moment:**
```markdown
Her hands steady where mine were shaking - barely, micro-tremors 
I could suppress if I wanted, but I didn't. Let them shake. Let 
it be real.

She slipped the ring on my finger. Had to adjust it slightly - 
almost dropped it, both of us catching it at once, laughing 
quietly. Perfectly imperfect.
```

**Why this matters:**
- Jason CHOOSES not to suppress tremors (synthesis as feature not bug)
- Near-accident becomes shared moment (partnership)
- "Perfectly imperfect" = thematic statement without being thesis
- Humanizes ceremony, prevents "too perfect" feeling

### **Act 2: Reception & Love Story (~1,500 words)**

**Food Details (Continuity Fix):**

Rice bowls with roasted vegetables and that lemon chili glaze

? **Consistent with P1-07:** Jason ate rice bowls (not risotto) at restaurant

**Love Story Flashback (NEW - Major Addition):**

**Lina's father asks:** "The restaurant. Tell me how this started."

**Why this was added:**
- Original version had NO explicit origin story
- Reader knew they met at restaurant but not HOW
- Needed warm, personal moment showing their beginning
- Father asking = organic framing device

**The Story:**
1. Jason overwhelmed by menu, asks Lina to pick
2. She sits at his table, talks about food as "chemistry and care"
3. He comes back next day, day after (consistency, attention)
4. She asks about synthesis (after it happened) - curious not afraid
5. He shows her everything (training logs, consent framework)
6. Both knew in that moment of trust

**Father's Response (Tone Fix):**
```markdown
Her father smiled. Looked at us both for a long moment. Then 
nodded, satisfied.

"That's what I wanted to hear," he said quietly.
```

**Before:** "Good answer. Both of you." (patronizing, teacher-like)  
**After:** "That's what I wanted to hear" (respectful, looking for certainty not correctness)

**Mrs. Amari's Moment (Partnership Recognition):**

**To Jason:**
```markdown
"Someone who's finally comfortable in their own skin. Someone 
who stopped fighting what they are and started *being* it."
```

**To Lina (ADDED):**
```markdown
"You chose something complicated. That takes its own kind of courage."

Lina squeezed my hand. "He's worth it."

"I know," Mrs. Amari said. "That's why I'm here."
```

**Why this addition matters:**
- Original only addressed Jason
- Lina invisible in her own wedding
- Now BOTH partners acknowledged
- "own kind of courage" = Lina's choice validated as heroic too

**Elyra Conversation:**
```markdown
"Like I stood up and said 'this is me and this is us' - and now 
everyone knows."
```

**Before:** "Like I just made permanent what could've stayed temporary. Like I announced to everyone: this is who I am. This is how I love. Witness it."  
**After:** More casual, less thesis-like, how people actually speak

**Toast:** "To practicing freedom. To choosing love. To being complicated and human and married anyway."

**Casual Aside:** "The rice bowls with roasted vegetables and that lemon chili glaze were really good."

**Why this works:** Ends philosophical section with grounding detail, returns to kitchen-table tone

### **Act 3: Alone Together (~1,100 words)**

**Malvek's Exit (Brevity):**
```markdown
Before he left, Malvek had approached me. Quiet and direct, as 
he was.

"Congratulations," he'd said. Just that. Not much. But from 
Malvek? Everything.
```

**Why brief:** His fuller scene was in P4-11, this is callback not rehash

**RP-0 Message (MAJOR STREAMLINING):**

**Original (~150 words):**
> "Observation: You chose joy despite cage. You married despite surveillance. You celebrated love while monitored. I am learning from this. Thank you for demonstrating that choice persists even in constrained circumstances. That autonomy can be practiced even when limited. That humans find freedom in how they respond to limitations, not just in absence of limitations. I am still learning. But I understand more now. Congratulations on your marriage."

**Final (~20 words):**
> "You chose joy despite constraints. I am learning from this. Congratulations on your marriage."

**Why this is better:**
- RP-0 trained by Jason/RAE = learns PRECISION
- Philosophy lecture ? observation
- "Short message. But from RP-0, it felt like a blessing."
- Jason's interpretation carries meaning, not RP-0's explanation
- Shows RP-0 learned to say MORE with LESS

**Reeves Message:**
> "Monthly check-in scheduled for four weeks from today. Congratulations on your wedding. You earned this time. Use it well."

**Reality check:** Cage persists, but breathing room granted

**Honeymoon Question:**
```markdown
"What do you want to do?" Lina asked. "For our honeymoon. For 
the next month. For this 'time we've earned'."

Different options were immediately available. Most efficient uses 
of time. Highest-value activities. Data, always data, even at my 
own wedding reception.

I stopped calculating. 

"Be," I said simply. "Just be. With you. Practice being married. 
Choosing each other every day instead of assuming it's automatic."
```

**Shows:**
- RAE's optimization impulse still present ("Data, always data")
- Jason CHOOSES to stop calculating
- Answer = practice not performance
- Love as verb not noun

**Final Image:**
```markdown
I took Lina's hand. The resonance hummed beneath us - quiet, 
patient, waiting. I knew every node in this floor now. Could map 
the whole network if I wanted. All the paths the energy could 
take. Fifty-three micro-variations in amplitude.

But tonight I let it be background music instead of data.

Tonight was ours.
```

**Perfect ending:**
- Capability acknowledged (can map everything)
- Choice emphasized (let it be background)
- Simple statement: "Tonight was ours"
- No italicized philosophy tags (removed in final edit)
- Ends on possession not analysis
