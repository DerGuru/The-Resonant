### **What Changed & Why It's Better:**

#### 1. **More Natural Flow**
**Before:** Formal explanation in one breath  
**After:** Fragmented, guilty confession

The "..." pause and fragmented structure shows Milo's discomfort. He's not explaining - he's *admitting*.

#### 2. **Lina's Role Clarified**
**New detail:** "Lina was keeping to it"

This adds:
- ? Lina was WITH Milo (established presence)
- ? She was monitoring/supporting (character consistency)
- ? They were working together (team dynamic)
- ? Milo wasn't alone in making decision (shared responsibility)

#### 3. **"I gave them your name - *Mr. Chen*"**
The italicized *Mr. Chen* with explicit "I gave them your name" emphasizes:
- Milo takes full responsibility
- He's being precise about what he shared
- The formality of "Mr. Chen" vs casual conversation = official context

#### 4. **Timeline Clarity**
**"when the ambulance picked you up"**

This specifies WHEN the information was given (during pickup, not hospital arrival), which:
- Makes it emergency context (no time to think)
- Shows pressure of moment
- Explains why he didn't consider consequences

---

### **Why This Version Is Superior:**

**V2 Strength:** Clear, logical, complete  
**V3 Strength:** Clear, logical, complete, **AND emotionally authentic**

The fragmented delivery ("... I gave them your number though") captures:
- Guilt (the pause)
