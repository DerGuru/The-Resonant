# Alpha Reader Feedback: P2-06 - "Max three channels. That's the limit."

## Overall Impression
**Rating: 7/10**

This chapter provides a pleasant interlude focused on practical tool-building and team development. The whisper clips are a smart addition to the magic system, and the chapter has a satisfying "training montage" feel. However, it suffers from timeline confusion and oddly abandons the major dramatic tension established in P2-05 about Jason's integration crisis.

## Critical Issue: Dropped Thread

### The Integration Crisis
**P2-05 ending**: Jason has 3-4 weeks before irreversible integration. He experienced terrifying/thrilling merged consciousness. He needs to make a life-altering decision. Elyra told him: "No active training. No invest work. Minimize resonance use."

**P2-06**: Opens "Three weeks into training with Elyra" with active training happening, Jason using invest heavily, and zero mention of the integration crisis or his decision.

**This is a major problem**. The previous chapter established urgent stakes and a ticking clock. This chapter acts like that never happened. Either:
1. Jason made his decision off-page (unacceptable - this is too important)
2. The timeline is confused (possible given the opening)
3. The author forgot about the crisis (hopefully not)

**Required fix**: Address the integration situation. Did Jason:
- Decide to stabilize at 40%?
- Decide to accept permanent coupling?
- Still struggling with the choice?
- Successfully maintain boundaries during this training?

This *must* be acknowledged.

## Timeline Confusion

### Opening Line Problem
"Three weeks into training with Elyra."

**Questions this raises**:
- Three weeks from when? First meeting? Last chapter?
- If from first meeting: We're past the integration crisis deadline (Jason had 3-4 weeks from P2-05)
- If from some other point: When?
- P2-05 ended with "no active training" - when did training resume?

**Previous timeline established**:
- P2-04: First session at Mill-4
- P2-05: "One week later... third training session"
- P2-06: "Three weeks into training"

This suggests we've skipped forward significantly, but no transition establishes what happened during that skip.

**Excellent addition**: The clips solve a practical problem and feel technologically consistent
- **Clear limitations**: 2 channels easy, 3 taxing, 4 too much
- **Grounded in magic system**: Relies on RAE + resonance frequency, not pure tech

- **Range testing**: Smart to establish limits immediately

This feels like natural problem-solving by intelligent characters.

### Team Development
- **Collaborative success**: Everyone contributes (Milo designs, Jason tests, Lina operationalizes)
- **Positive momentum**: After intense chapters, a win feels earned
- **Competence building**: "Getting competent" - realistic self-assessment
- **Group cohesion**: The team is gelling nicely

### Elyra's Teaching Evolution
- **Engaged rather than distant**: She's invested in their success
- **Practical focus**: "Until using them becomes automatic"
- **Divided attention drill**: Logical next step in training progression
- **Encouraging**: "Good work" - she's warming to them

### Tone
The chapter has a lighter, more optimistic tone after several intense chapters. This is good pacing - readers need breathing room. The "pleasant exhaustion of a day well spent" captures this well.

## Weaknesses

### Pacing and Structure
**Too compressed**: The chapter covers:
- Initial concept discussion
- Multiple prototype iterations
- Range testing
- Protocol establishment
- Next day's divided attention training
- All in a short chapter

This feels like it should span 2-3 chapters or be expanded significantly. Major developments (successful whisper clips!) are rushed through.

### Missing Dramatic Tension
Unlike every previous chapter, there's no conflict, no failure, no setback. Everything works. This is pleasant but lacks narrative drive.

**Consider adding**:
- A prototype fails dangerously
- The clips work but have an unexpected side effect
- Someone else detects their communication
- Integration level spikes during heavy RAE usage

### Character Development
**Jason's internal state**: The integration crisis should be affecting him psychologically. Does he:
- Feel the integration deepening during clip work?
- Monitor his percentage anxiously?
- Experience moments of boundary blur?
- Worry about the choice he's making/avoiding?

None of this appears. He's just... happy and tired.

### Milo's Expanded Role

Consider: Brief mention of his background (engineering hobbyist? Academy tech support?) to ground this expertise.

## Bottom Line

This is a pleasant, competently written chapter that develops useful tools and shows team growth. However, it critically fails to address the integration crisis established in P2-05 and suffers from timeline confusion. The lack of dramatic tension makes it feel like filler despite introducing useful elements.

**Primary recommendation**:
1. **URGENT**: Address Jason's integration status and decision-making process
2. Clarify timeline relative to P2-05's deadline
3. Add at least one complication or failure to create dramatic interest
4. Connect to ongoing plot threads (investigation, watchers, etc.)

**Secondary recommendations**:
- Expand the chapter to give whisper clip development proper weight
- Show Elyra checking Jason's integration level
- Add brief mention of external world consequences
- Include character conflict or uncertainty alongside success
