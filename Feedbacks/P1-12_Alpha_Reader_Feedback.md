### ? The Title "A question that's still alive"

**Appears in Milo's pivotal speech:**

> "Because I've spent three years asking questions nobody wants to answer. Digging through suppressed records, following dead ends, trying to understand what happened eleven years ago. And now you show up, careful and scared and clearly in over your head, and I think: maybe this is what I've been looking for. Not answers. A question that's still alive."

**Why this title is perfect:**

1. **Captures Milo's motivation** - He's been chasing dead ends, now finds living mystery
2. **RAE is literally alive** - "Question" = RAE, "still alive" = not archived data
3. **Philosophical depth** - Seeking questions vs. having answers
4. **Differentiates research** - Living investigation vs. historical study
5. **Thematic weight** - About discovery, not closure
6. **Poetic without pretension** - Beautiful phrasing, clear meaning
7. **Sets partnership tone** - They're exploring together, not solving definitively

**The title captures what makes this meeting special: Jason brings a living mystery, Milo brings obsessive curiosity.**
