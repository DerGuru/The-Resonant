## The Journey: Three Attempts

### Attempt One: The Vision Without Direction

I started with a rough concept and a desired tone. I wanted the main character to be a blend of Jason Asano (from *He Who Fights With Monsters*) and Fisher (from *Heretical Fishing*), with a story progression similar to *Beware of Chickens*.

It didn't work.

The AI produced generic text with an overly mystical tone. The characters felt hollow. The world felt stale and I realized I needed more structure.

### Attempt Two: The Outline That Stalled

For the second attempt, I created a detailed outline first. The first part came together well𤪳ut by Part Two, the story stopped progressing. The outline had become a cage rather than a roadmap. I lost momentum, lost interest.

I fed what I had to another AI for feedback.

The verdict? **BAD.** Capital-letter BAD瑈ot Part One, but everything after.

I almost gave up.

### Attempt Three: The Breakthrough

Instead of starting over, I asked the AI to make it better㻡nd it actually did.

It took most of Part One and condensed it. Reordered chapters. Restructured scenes within and between chapters. Cut bloat. Sharpened focus.

It was *much better*.

My drive returned. I wanted to read this story again瑈ot as a creator struggling with craft, but as a reader eager to see what happens next.

## What This Process Actually Looked Like

Working with AI to write a novel isn't as simple as typing "Tell me a story" and waiting for a manuscript.

It's a process. A demanding one.

**Keeping the AI on track:** Linear thinking isn't its strength. It forgets details, loses plot threads, contradicts itself. I had to be vigilant.

**Keeping characters in character:** The AI would sometimes flatten personalities or have characters act inconsistently. I had to intervene constantly.

**Fine-tuning dialogue and scenes:** The AI's first draft of a conversation might be functional, but lifeless. I rewrote dialogue for rhythm, subtext, voice.

**Reordering scenes:** Sometimes the AI's suggested sequence didn't create the right emotional or narrative flow. I rearranged, restructured, rebuilt.

**Writing sections myself:** When the AI couldn't capture what I wanted㻡 specific tone, a subtle character moment, a complex emotional beat鎸 wrote it by hand.

This isn't "AI-generated content" you can dismiss.

This isn't "editing and proofreading" you can reduce to cleanup.

This is **collaboration.**

So I'd stop. Prompt the AI. Sometimes that fixed it immediately. More often, I had to give precise instructions: "Make this sharper." "Cut this section." "Show the emotion, don't tell it."

And sometimes, I had to take over completely𤪳ecause the AI just couldn't deliver what I envisioned.
