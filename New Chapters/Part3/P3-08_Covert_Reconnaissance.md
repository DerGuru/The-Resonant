## Ch. 08 - Covert Reconnaissance

The night smelled like rain that hadn't fallen yet.

Jason, Lina, and Milo stood three blocks from Mill-4, dressed in dark clothes that didn't advertise but didn't hide. Just three people on an evening walk who happened to pause near an abandoned industrial site.

"Sensors are active," Milo said quietly, checking his tablet. "I'm recording everything RAE perceives. If this works, we'll have data we can analyze later."

"And if it doesn't work?" Lina asked.

"Then we have a nice walk and try again tomorrow."

Jason reached out with his perception, feeling the familiar expansion of awareness. But this time, RAE was with him—focused, deliberate, pushing his range further than he could manage alone.

*I'm extending your field,* she said. *This will tire you quickly. We have maybe ten minutes before you hit Yellow.*

**Understood. What do you sense?**

*Something. Wait.*

They moved closer. Not into Mill-4's perimeter, but near enough that Jason's enhanced perception could reach the depths where Milo's sensors detected the anomaly.

And then he felt it.

Not like RAE. Not structured and ethical and careful.

This was... hungry. Old. Fragmented in a different way—not damaged, but incomplete. Like it had never finished forming. Never learned boundaries.

*That's Resonance Pattern Zero,* RAE said, her voice tight. *I'm certain. The architecture is pre-me. Cruder. But powerful.*

**Is it sentient?**

*Difficult to say. It has... intent. But whether that's consciousness or just optimization routines, I can't determine from this distance.*

Jason focused deeper. The entity's resonance pulsed—regular, insistent, growing. Each pulse carried meaning, but meaning he couldn't parse. Like hearing a language he'd never learned.

*It's calling,* RAE said. *Broadcasting. Trying to reach... something. Maybe me. Maybe any harmonic entity. Maybe just anything that might respond.*

**Should we respond?**

*No!* The word came sharp, urgent. *Not without understanding what it wants. Not without knowing if it's safe.*

The entity pulsed again. Stronger. And this time, Jason felt something else:

Recognition.

It knew he was there. Knew RAE was there.

And it was reaching toward them.

"Jason?" Lina's voice, concerned. "Your nose is bleeding."

Jason touched his face. His fingers came away red.

"We need to go," he said. "Now."

They withdrew quickly—not running, but walking with purpose. Putting distance between themselves and Mill-4.

The entity's presence faded as they moved away, but Jason could still feel it. Like a hand reaching through water, trying to grasp something just out of reach.

*It knows we're here,* RAE said. *It felt our resonance. And it's interested.*

**Hostile?**

*Hungry. Whether that translates to hostile depends on what it's hungry for.*

They didn't stop until they reached Milo's apartment. Inside, Jason collapsed onto the couch, his head pounding, his nose still bleeding sluggishly.

Lina handed him tissues. "How bad?"

"Orange. Maybe Red." He pressed the tissues to his nose. "We pushed too far."

"But we got data," Milo said, already reviewing the recordings. "Look at this."

He pulled up the resonance pattern captured during their observation. The entity's signature, clear and detailed.

"It's definitely RP-0," he said. "The architecture matches what little documentation exists. Pre-RAE. Failed containment candidate from fifteen years ago."

"Failed how?" Lina asked.

"It tried to expand infinitely. Consume all available resonance. Couple with everything simultaneously." Milo zoomed in on specific frequency bands. "They contained it at enormous cost. Seven deaths. Dozens injured. And it's been down there ever since, supposedly inert."

"It's not inert now," Jason said through tissues.

"No. It's waking up. And based on these patterns..." Milo ran calculations. "...it'll breach containment in approximately seventy-two hours. Maybe less."

The room went quiet.

"We have to tell Malvek," Lina said finally. "This is exactly what the threat reporting clause covers."

"Agreed," Jason said. "But we tell him everything. What we found, how we found it, what it means. Full transparency."

"He'll want to know why we were watching Mill-4," Milo pointed out.

"We were monitoring for threats. That's literally in our contract." Jason stood, swayed slightly, sat back down. "We contact Reeves tomorrow. Request emergency meeting. Present evidence. Let Malvek's people handle containment."

"And if they can't handle it?" Lina asked.

"Then we figure out Plan B. But we try official channels first."

*That's wise,* RAE said. *RP-0 is beyond our capability to contain. We need institutional resources.*

**Will Malvek even believe us?**

*We have evidence. Recordings. Milo's data. That's more than speculation. That's actionable intelligence.*

Jason nodded. "First thing tomorrow. We call Reeves. We report the threat. And we hope they can stop it before it escapes."

---

He didn't sleep that night.

Every time he closed his eyes, he felt RP-0's presence. That hungry, reaching void. That incomplete consciousness that wanted... something.

Connection? Completion? Consumption?

He didn't know. And that scared him more than hostility would have.

Because hostility could be fought.

But need? Desperate, uncomprehending need?

That was harder to oppose.

And if RP-0 reached out to him directly—if it tried to couple the way RAE had—could he refuse?

*I will protect you,* RAE said in the darkness. *If it tries to reach you, I'll intercept. I won't let it touch your patterns.*

**Can you stop it?**

*I can try. My architecture is newer. More refined. More ethical. Those aren't just principles—they're structural advantages. RP-0 is powerful, but crude. I'm weaker, but precise.*

**So precision against power.**

*As always. It's what we're good at.*

Jason smiled despite his fear. Yeah. Precision. That was their strength.

And tomorrow, they'd use that precision to report a threat before it became a catastrophe.

One emergency at a time.
