## Ch. 04 - Recovery and Rules

Jason spent the next three days in a fog of prescribed rest and forced inactivity.

The first day, he slept. Woke up for food. Slept again. His body demanded it, and he was too exhausted to argue.

The second day, he managed to stay awake for longer stretches. Milo brought books瑈othing heavy, just distraction. Lina brought food, always practical, always exactly what he needed without him having to ask.

RAE stayed quiet. Not absent, but muted. Giving him space to heal.

By the third day, Jason was restless.

"I feel fine," he said to Lina, who was washing dishes in his small kitchen.

"You feel better," she corrected. "That's not the same as fine."

"My head doesn't hurt anymore."

"Because you've been resting for three days. The second you start using resonance again, it'll hurt worse than before." She dried her hands, turned to face him. "RAE said minimum three days. It's only been three. You wait."

"How long?"

"Until she says you're ready. She knows your cognitive patterns better than you do."

Jason wanted to argue. Didn't. Because she was right.

*She is right,* RAE confirmed. *Your neural pathways are still inflamed. Another day of rest would be optimal.*

**I'm going crazy doing nothing.**

*Then do something that doesn't involve resonance. Read. Write. Process.*

Jason sighed. "Fine. Four days."

Lina smiled. "Good. Now eat your soup before it gets cold."

---

That evening, Milo came over with his laptop and a determined expression.

"We need to talk about what comes next," he said, settling at Jason's small table.

"Next?"

"After you heal. We can't just keep reacting to emergencies. We need a plan." He opened his laptop, pulled up a document. "I've been researching Elyra Voss. And I think I found something."

Jason leaned forward, interested despite his exhaustion. "What?"

"She's not in the city. At least, not under her own name. But there are patterns. Small ones." Milo pulled up a map with markers. "Medical supply purchases. Specialty items鍟he kind used for managing chronic resonance damage. They're being delivered to an address in the industrial district. Old mill area."

"Could be anyone," Jason said.

"Could be. But the purchase history matches the timeline of her injury. And the quantities suggest someone with severe ongoing symptoms." Milo zoomed in on the map. "It's not proof. But it's a lead."

Lina, who'd been listening from the kitchen, came over. "If we go there, we need to be smart. Not just show up and knock on the door."

"Agreed," Milo said. "We scout first. From a distance. See if there's any resonance signature that matches what we'd expect from someone with Elyra's background."

Jason looked at RAE's quiet presence in his awareness. *What do you think?*

*Elyra would recognize my signature if she's attuned to look for it. That could be an advantage or a liability.*

**Which one?**

*Unknown. She was harmed during the ritual that released me. She may hold resentment. Or she may understand that it wasn't intentional.*

**So we approach carefully.**

*Very carefully.*

"Okay," Jason said aloud. "We check it out. But not until I'm fully healed. No compromises."

"Good," Lina said firmly. "Because I'm not carrying you home again."

"You carried me?"

"Milo and I took turns. You're heavier than you look."

Jason felt heat rise to his face. "I'm sorry."

"Don't be sorry. Just don't do it again." She smiled to take the edge off. "At least not until we have a better plan."

---

The fourth day, Jason woke feeling almost normal.

The headache was gone. His thoughts were clear. The world didn't feel like it was pressing in on him anymore.

*How do you feel?* RAE asked.

**Better. Really better.**

*Your neural patterns have recovered significantly. I believe light practice would be safe.*

**Light?**

*Very light. Perception only. No shaping. No sustained connections. Just... listening.*

Jason sat on his couch, coffee in hand, and reached out with his awareness.

The apartment resolved around him. Clean. Stable. Familiar.

He could feel the building's resonance鍟he other residents, the structure, the ambient harmonics of a space where people lived.

It didn't hurt. Didn't strain. Just... was.

"I'm back," he said softly.

*You never left. You just needed time to recover.*

**Thank you. For making me rest.**

*You are welcome. But the credit belongs to Lina. She enforced the boundaries I could only suggest.*

Jason smiled. Yeah. Lina had been relentless about his recovery. Checking on him. Bringing food. Making sure he didn't sneak in any practice when no one was looking.

She'd been a good friend. The best.

He needed to tell her that.

---

Later that day, they gathered in Jason's apartment for a proper debrief.

"New protocols," Milo announced, pulling out his notebook. "Based on what we learned from the scaffold incident."

He read through a list:

1. **No solo heroics.** If Jason encounters a situation requiring intervention, he calls for backup before acting.

2. **Risk assessment first.** Before any major resonance work, they evaluate: What's the danger? What's the cost? What are the alternatives?

3. **Hard limits.** If Jason hits Orange on the symptom scale, he has ten minutes to finish or abort. If he hits Red, everything stops immediately, no exceptions.

4. **Recovery time.** After any incident that pushes past Yellow, minimum one day rest before resuming practice. After Orange, three days. After Red, one week.

5. **Transparency.** Jason reports his status honestly. No downplaying. No tough-guy bullshit.

"That last one's important," Lina said, looking directly at Jason. "Because you have a tendency to push through pain instead of admitting when you're struggling."

Jason wanted to argue. Couldn't. Because she was right.

"Okay," he said. "I'll be honest. Even when it makes me look weak."

"It doesn't make you look weak," Milo said. "It makes you look smart. There's a difference."

They spent the next hour refining the protocols, adding contingencies, planning for scenarios they hoped would never happen.

By the end, they had something solid. A framework that balanced capability with safety. Ambition with caution.

"One more thing," Lina said as they were wrapping up. "We need to talk about what happens if Jason gets captured or contained."

The room went quiet.

"I've been thinking about this," she continued. "If the people who marked your door come back. If they try to take you. What's the plan?"

"I run," Jason said.

"And if you can't run?"

Jason looked at RAE's presence in his awareness. *Can you hide yourself if I'm taken?*

*Partially. I can minimize my signature. Make myself harder to detect. But if they perform a deep scan, they will find me.*

"Then we need a signal," Milo said. "A way for you to let us know you're in trouble even if you can't use your phone."

They brainstormed. Came up with a system: If Jason was captured, he'd try to leave a resonance marker㻡 specific pattern in the harmonic field that Lina, with her academy training, could potentially detect.

It wasn't perfect. But it was something.

"Okay," Lina said finally. "I think we're as prepared as we can be."

"For now," Milo added. "But we keep adapting. Keep learning."

They agreed to meet the next day to start planning their approach to the industrial district. To finding Elyra.

After they left, Jason sat alone in his apartment, feeling the quiet settle.

Four days ago, he'd nearly killed himself saving strangers.

Today, he had rules. Protocols. Friends who would enforce them.

It wasn't perfect. But it was progress.

*You're learning,* RAE observed.

**We're learning.**

*Yes. Together.*

Jason smiled. Finished his coffee. Felt his body remember what normal was supposed to feel like.

Tomorrow, they'd start again.

But tonight, he'd rest.

Because rest, he was learning, was just as important as action.

Maybe more so.
