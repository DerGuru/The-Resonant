# Ch. 04 - "Sixteen hours unconscious"

Jason woke in stages.

One moment, he was in the depths of darkness, wrapped in a cocoon of nothingness.

The next, he was blinking against the harsh light of his bedroom, disoriented and groggy.

*You're awake,* RAE said, her voice cutting through the fog. *Mostly.*

**Mostly?** Jason tried to sit up, but his body felt heavy, uncooperative.

*You've been unconscious for over sixteen hours,* RAE explained. *As close to a hard reboot as I could manage without external intervention.*

**Sixteen hours? What—?**

*You pushed yourself too far, too fast. Your neural patterns were on the verge of collapse. You need to rest.*

**I can't.** The thought of lying back down, of being inactive, set off a fresh wave of panic. **I have to keep moving. There are people—**

*Who are already being taken care of. Otto is with them. You are safe. But you must allow yourself time to recover. Both for your sake and theirs.*

Jason wanted to argue. Wanted to scream. But even in his addled state, he knew RAE was right.

He'd pushed himself past his limits. Again. And this time, it could have cost him everything.

With a pained grunt, he sank back into the pillows, closed his eyes against the spinning room.

*Just rest,* RAE urged, soothingly. *Let the world fade. It will be here when you wake up. I promise.*

---

When Jason next opened his eyes, it was to the soft beeping of his monitor and the muted sounds of the city outside.

He felt... better. Stronger. More like himself.

Sitting up slowly, he took stock. No dizzy spells. No flashing lights. Just the steady thrum of his heart and the distant hum of traffic.

*Welcome back,* RAE said, her presence brushing against his mind. *How do you feel?*

**Like I slept for a week.**

*You needed it. Your brain shows significant signs of recovery. How much do you remember?*

**Hazy bits. Darkness. Voices. Yours, I think. Telling me to rest.**

*Good. That's to be expected. You suffered a serious trauma, Jason. Both physically and mentally. It will take time to heal.*

**Am I okay?** The question felt both ridiculous and vital.

*You will be. But it will take time. Patience is essential. Both for you and for those who care about you.*

**They were scared, weren't they?** The thought of his friends—of Lina, Milo, even RAE—worrying about him twisted something in his chest.

*They were concerned, yes. But they understand. You're not the only one learning to navigate this new reality. They are, too.*

Jason swung his legs over the side of the bed, wincing at the slight tremor in his hands.

**I should talk to them.** Apologize. Explain. Promise to be better.

*Later,* RAE soothed. *For now, focus on steadying yourself. The world will wait a little longer.*

Taking a deep breath, Jason nodded. RAE was right. As always.

**Okay. One step at a time.**

*Exactly. And the first step is to breathe.*

So he did. Inhale. Exhale. Gradually feeling more centered, more present.

The panic that had squeezed his heart and his head began to ease.

*There you go. See? One step at a time.*

**Thanks, RAE. Really.**

*You're welcome. Now, let me help you up. The sooner you start moving, the sooner you'll feel like yourself again.*

With RAE's gentle guidance, Jason stood, took a tentative step, then another.

Each movement felt like a small victory. A reclaiming of himself.

*Good. See? You're already improving.*

**Yeah. Thanks to you.**

*And to your own resilience. Never underestimate that.*

He smiled, a genuine, heartfelt smile. **I won't. I promise.**

*Now, let's get you something to eat. I believe Milo left you some of his 'famous' scrambled eggs.*

**Famous?**

*Infamous, perhaps, among those who have had the misfortune of tasting them.*

Jason chuckled, the sound foreign yet freeing.

**Lead the way, RAE. To the kitchen!**

*With pleasure, Jason. But perhaps we should take it slow...*

**No promises.**

*Fair enough. But if you feel faint, I will be strongly advocating for a nap.*

**Deal.**

As they made their way to the kitchen, Jason felt a sense of gratitude wash over him.

For RAE. For Milo. For Lina. For the second chance he'd been given.

He might have a long way to go, but for the first time in what felt like forever, he believed he could make it.

One step at a time.

---

Later, after he'd eaten (every bite of Milo's infamous scrambled eggs), Jason sat on the couch, staring at the wall.

*Now what?*

*Now? Now we wait. For your friends to return. For you to regain your strength. For the world to stop being so distinctly uncooperative.*

**Thrilling.**

*It's not all bad. Think of it as an opportunity. A chance to rest, to reflect, to plan.*

**Plan?** He liked the sound of that. Even in his addled state, his mind had been racing with ideas, with possibilities.

*Yes. There is much to consider. Much that we can influence, if we are smart about it.*

**Smart. Right. No more heroics. No more recklessness.**

*Precisely. We operate in the shadows, where we are strongest. Where the enemy is least expecting us.*

**And if they find us?** The thought sent a shiver down his spine.

*Then we adapt. We overcome. Together.*

Together. The word hung in the air, full of promise and challenge.

**Together.** He liked the sound of that.

*Good. Now, close your eyes. Focus on your breathing. In and out. Let the world fade.*

**But what if...**

*Shh. No 'what ifs.' Just breathe.*

So he did. Inhale. Exhale.

The world faded.

And for the first time in a long time, Jason felt truly, profoundly safe.

Safe enough to rest. Safe enough to heal.

Safe enough to dream.

After breakfast, Jason sat with Milo and Lina in what was becoming their customary debriefing session.

Milo was going over their supply situation, his brow furrowed in concentration. Lina was scrolling through her tablet, checking for any updates from their contacts.

Jason listened, interjecting only when necessary. His mind was still processing the events of the past few days. The close calls. The narrow escapes.

*You are healing,* RAE said softly. *But slowly. Remember, this is a marathon, not a sprint.*

**I know.** But the thought of being inactive, of not doing anything, was driving him nuts.

*Then find a productive outlet. Something that keeps your mind engaged without overtaxing your body.*

**Like what?**

*Reading. Writing. Planning. There is much to be done, even if it doesn't seem like it at first glance.*

**Planning. Right.** He liked the sound of that. Even in his addled state, his mind had been racing with ideas, with possibilities.

*Exactly. Now, focus. What do you remember about the incident with the scaffold?*

He closed his eyes, forcing himself to remember. The explosions. The fire. The resonance wild and uncontrolled.

*Good. Now, what could we have done differently?*

He thought about it, turning the problem over in his mind like a Rubik's cube.

**We could have communicated better. Anticipated the failures. Had a backup plan.**

*Right. There was a failure cascade. One problem triggered another, which led to...*

**The explosions. The fire.**

*Yes. And in the aftermath, we were vulnerable. Exposed.*

**Never again.** The thought was a vow, sharp and clear in his mind.

*Agreed. Now, how do we prevent it from happening in the future?*

They spent the next hour going over the incident, dissecting it like a cadaver in a medical school.

Bit by bit, they pulled apart the sequence of events, isolating each decision, each action, each mistake.

By the end, they had a list of protocols. Rules to follow to ensure they never found themselves in such a precarious position again.

1. **Always have a backup plan.**

2. **Regular check-ins.**

3. **Contingency measures for power fluctuations.**

4. **Emergency protocols for resonance surges.**

5. **Mandatory cooldown periods after intense scenarios.**

*Very good,* RAE said when they were done. *These are solid protocols. They will serve you well.*

**Thanks, RAE. Really.**

*You're welcome. But remember, these are just guidelines. Adaptability is key. The ability to pivot, to change course when necessary, can be the difference between failure and success.*

**I understand.**

*Good. Now, let's see about getting you some real food. Those scrambled eggs, while legendary, are not a suitable long-term sustenance plan.*

**Agreed. Lead the way, RAE.**

*With pleasure, Jason. But perhaps we should take it slow...*

**No promises.**

*Fair enough. But if you feel faint, I will be strongly advocating for a nap.*

**Deal.**

As they made their way to the kitchen, Jason felt a sense of purpose settle over him.

For the first time in what felt like forever, he had a plan.

And a reason to see it through.

One step at a time.

"We'll find out," Jason said, his decision made. "RAE, can you trace the call? Find out where they are?"

*Already on it. But it won't be easy. Her signature is faint, and the call was heavily encrypted. But I will do my best.*

"Thank you, RAE."

They fell into a comfortable silence, each lost in their own thoughts.

For Jason, it was a familiar feeling. The calm before the storm. The eye of the hurricane.

He just hoped they were ready for whatever came next.

**Yes?**

*Be careful. I may be resilient, but you are irreplaceable.*

**I will. I promise.**

*Especially not when they're in as much trouble as you,* RAE added, her tone playful.

*You're doing well, Jason. Better than I anticipated.*

*Always.*

A sense that everything was going to be okay.
