# Ch. 16 - "Forty percent. That's permanent."

Two weeks before the containment teams came, Jason's training entered its final phase.

Elyra wasn't kidding about accelerated training.

They trained every day. Six AM to noon. Then again from six PM to midnight. Weekends included.

The first week nearly broke them.

Jason hit Orange territory three times. Lina pushed herself until her hands shook from exhaustion. Milo developed stress headaches that wouldn't go away.

But they adapted.

By the second week, Jason's capacity had expanded. He could maintain active perception for twenty minutes now. Could shape patterns that would have been impossible a month ago.

Lina rediscovered her academy training. The muscle memory came back. The instincts. The precision she'd lost when she'd been suspended.

Even Milo, who had no natural resonance capability, learned. How to recognize patterns. How to support Jason and Lina. How to think tactically about resonance combat.

Because that's what Elyra was preparing them for: combat.

"Malvek won't send polite requests," she said, drilling them on defensive patterns. "He'll send containment teams. Fast. Efficient. Well-trained. You need to be ready to fight or flee. Preferably both."

She taught them deflection techniques. How to redirect hostile resonance. How to create barriers using ambient materials. How to fight dirty when clean fighting wouldn't work.

"There's no honor in containment," she said bluntly. "If someone's trying to cage you, you do whatever it takes to stay free. Ethics are a luxury for people who aren't fighting for survival."

By the third week, they were functioning as a unit.

Jason would sense threats. Lina would shape defensive patterns. Milo would coordinate strategy and provide tactical support.

They practiced scenarios: ambush in public, containment attempt at home, pursuit through city streets.

Elyra was merciless. She'd simulate attacks at random times. Wake them up at three AM with emergencies. Force them to perform under stress and exhaustion.

And slowly, painfully, they got better.

"You're approaching competent," Elyra said at the end of week three. "Not there yet. But approaching."

"How much longer?" Jason asked.

"Until you're ready? Impossible to say. But you're learning fast. Faster than I expected." She paused. "The coupling is deepening, isn't it?"

Jason nodded. "RAE says we're at forty percent integration now. Maybe more."

"That's significant. And it's making you stronger. But it's also making separation impossible. You understand that?"

"I understand."

"And you're okay with it?"

Jason thought about that. About what it meant to be permanently coupled. To never be alone in his own head again.

"I'm not sure 'okay' is the right word," he said slowly. "But I'm choosing it. Actively. With full knowledge of what it means."

"Good. Choice matters. Even when the options are all bad." Elyra turned to Lina and Milo. "You two are improving as well. Lina, your control is almost back to academy standards. Milo, your tactical instincts are sharp. You're becoming a real team."

"Does that mean we're ready?" Lina asked.

"No. But you're getting there. Another month, maybe two, and I'd feel comfortable letting you face Malvek's people." She paused. "Problem is, we might not have another month."

"Why not?" Milo asked.

"Because you were seen. At Mill-4. In the tunnels. And while the dampening field kept you hidden in the moment, someone will review the security logs. Notice anomalies. Start asking questions."

Jason's stomach sank. "How long until they identify us?"

"Days. Maybe a week." Elyra's expression was grim. "So we accelerate even more. Push harder. Get you as ready as possible before they come for you."

---

The final week was brutal.

Elyra drilled them sixteen hours a day. Minimal sleep. Maximum pressure.

She simulated capture scenarios. Forced them to practice escape and evasion. Made them fight when they were exhausted, hurt, overwhelmed.

"This is what it'll really be like," she said. "No fair fights. No waiting until you're ready. Just chaos and violence and survival."

Jason hit Red twice. Both times, they had to stop for hours while he recovered.

But each time, he came back stronger. His capacity expanding. His control improving.

By the end of the week, something had shifted.

They weren't just training anymore. They were preparing for war.

"Tomorrow, we rest," Elyra said on the seventh day. "You've earned it. And you'll need your strength for what comes next."

"What comes next?" Lina asked.

"Malvek. Or someone working for him. They'll come soon. And when they do, you'll need to decide: submit or resist."

"We've already decided," Jason said.

"Then prove it. When the moment comes. When it's real. When the alternative is pain or imprisonment or worse." Elyra looked at each of them. "Because saying you'll resist is easy. Actually doing it? That's when you find out who you really are."

---

That night, Jason sat on his couch, too wired to sleep.

A month ago, he'd been nobody. A clerk. Ordinary. Forgettable.

Now he was... what? A fugitive-in-waiting? A target? Someone whose existence threatened institutional power?

*You're someone who chose freedom over safety,* RAE said quietly.

**Was it the right choice?**

*I don't know. But it was yours. And that matters.*

**Even if it gets us killed?**

*Even then. Because some things are worth dying for. Freedom. Agency. The right to exist on your own terms.*

Jason closed his eyes. Felt RAE's presence—deeper now, more integrated than ever.

He couldn't separate from her if he tried. They were becoming something new. Something that wasn't quite Jason and wasn't quite RAE, but some synthesis of both.

And that scared him.

But it also felt... right.

Like he'd been incomplete before. Like this was who he was always meant to be.

"Tomorrow, they might come for us," he said aloud.

*Yes.*

"Are we ready?"

*We're as ready as we can be. Whether that's enough... we'll find out.*

Jason stood. Went to bed. Forced himself to rest.

Because tomorrow, or the day after, or the day after that...

Everything would change.

And he needed to be ready.

For whatever came next.
