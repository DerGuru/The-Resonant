## Ch. 07 - The Whisper Clips

"We need better communication," Milo said, spreading components across Elyra's table. "Phone calls are compromised. RAE speaking through random objects is conspicuous. We need something purpose-built."

It was their fifth day of training. Elyra had been drilling them on perception and control, but today she'd agreed to help with practical applications.

"Whisper clips," she said, examining Milo's sketches. "Old concept. Never worked reliably."

"Why not?" Lina asked.

"Because most people tried to make them do too much. Active transmission, encryption, range extension. All that complexity meant more failure points." She picked up one of Milo's prototypes㻡 clear plastic ear hook with a tiny ceramic disc embedded. "This is better. Simple. Passive. Relies on RAE's ability rather than trying to replicate it mechanically."

"So it'll work?" Milo asked hopefully.

"Probably. Let's test it."

They spent the morning calibrating. Jason wearing the first prototype, RAE attempting to project her voice directly to the clip. It took dozens of tries to find the right frequency, the right resonance point.

But finally:

"Can you hear me?" RAE's voice came through clear and private, audible only to Jason.

"Yes," he said aloud. Then, quieter: "This is weird."

"Weird but functional," Elyra said. "Now we make three more. One for each of you."

Building the clips took the rest of the day. Each one had to be tuned to its wearer's specific resonance frequency. Lina's was slightly different from Jason's. Milo's was different again.

But by evening, they had working prototypes.

"Test them outside my building," Elyra said. "Establish range limits. Interference patterns. Duration before fatigue sets in. Then report back tomorrow."

They did.

Standing in the industrial lot, spaced thirty meters apart, they tested.

RAE's voice came through each clip clearly: "Can you all hear me?"

"Yes," Jason said.

"Affirmative," Milo replied.

"Loud and clear," Lina confirmed.

They experimented. Found that RAE could maintain two simultaneous channels easily. Three was possible but taxing. Four would be too much without significant Invest from Jason.

"So we're a triangle," Milo said. "Three-person mesh. Anything more and we need to plan carefully."

"That works," Lina said. "Most operations won't need more than three anyway."

They established protocols:

- Floor control: only one person speaks at a time unless emergency
- Hand signals for channel requests
- RAE can interrupt anyone, anytime for warnings
- If Jason hits Orange, all channels close immediately

By the time they finished testing, the sun was setting and Jason was solidly in Yellow territory.

"That's enough for today," Lina decreed. "We report to Elyra tomorrow, then rest."

---

The next morning, Elyra examined their notes with approval.

"Good work. The clips are functional. Now you need to practice until using them becomes automatic." She set out three ceramic bowls. "Today's lesson: divided attention. You're going to maintain awareness of your environment while communicating through the mesh."

She wasn't gentle about it.

For two hours, she drilled them. Rapid-fire questions through the clips while simultaneously requiring them to sense and report on objects she moved around the room.

It was exhausting. Confusing. By the end, all three of them were making mistakes.

"Better," Elyra said. "You're learning to split focus without splitting concentration. Keep practicing. In a month, this should feel natural."

"A month?" Milo groaned.

"A month if you're diligent. Longer if you're lazy." She pointed at the door. "Go. Rest. Tomorrow we work on sustained channel duration."

They left, tired but pleased with their progress.

"We're actually getting good at this," Milo said on the tram ride home.

"We're getting competent," Lina corrected. "Good is still a long way off."

"I'll take competent," Jason said. "It's better than improvising blind."

They parted at Jason's stop. He climbed the stairs to his apartment, feeling the pleasant exhaustion of a day well spent.

Inside, he collapsed on his couch and pulled out his new whisper clip, examining it.

Such a small thing. Clear plastic and ceramic. Barely visible when worn.

But it represented something important: progress. Planning. The team becoming more capable with each day.

*You're proud,* RAE observed.

**Yeah. We built something useful today.**

*We did. And tomorrow, we'll build on it further.*

Jason smiled, closed his eyes, and let himself rest.

Tomorrow would bring new challenges. New lessons.

But tonight, this moment of satisfaction was enough.

They were learning. Growing. Becoming something more than they'd been.

One small step at a time.
