# Ch. 12 - "Destroy everything"

The screen went blank.

Not deleted. Not corrupted. Just... gone. As if the files had never existed.

Jason stared at his terminal, his blood turning cold. Every archive entry he'd flagged over the past three weeks. Every cross-reference to the Voss incident. Every breadcrumb trail Milo had helped him identify.

Systematically erased.

"RAE?" he said quietly.

*I see it. This was professional. Surgical. They removed specific entries while leaving surrounding records intact. To anyone not looking for them, it would appear normal.*

**Can we recover them?**

*Perhaps. If you had offline backups.*

Jason's stomach sank. He did have backups. On his encrypted drive at home.

But that meant someone had accessed the municipal archive system. With enough clearance to delete protected records. Without leaving obvious traces.

That was Malvek-level access.

He logged out carefully. Stood. Walked to the break room as if nothing was wrong.

In the hallway, he pulled out his phone. Texted Milo: *"They hit the archive. Everything we flagged is gone. Need to meet."*

The response came immediately: *"Checking my backups. Library. 30 minutes."*

Jason made his way out of the building, hyperaware of everyone around him. Harris at his desk. The cleaning crew. The security guard who nodded as Jason passed.

Any of them could be watching. Any of them could have reported his activity.

Or none of them. Maybe it was automatic—flagged searches triggering automated responses.

Either way, someone knew what he'd been researching. And they'd acted to stop him.

---

The library felt safer. Public. Neutral.

Milo had his laptop open, expression grim. "They hit me too. My cloud storage. The backups I thought were secure. All the Voss-related material. Gone."

"How?" Lina asked.

"Resonance signature tracking, maybe. Or they compromised my device. Or—" He shook his head. "Doesn't matter how. What matters is they're cleaning up. Removing evidence. Making sure nobody can prove what really happened eleven years ago."

"Did they get everything?" Jason asked.

"Not everything." Milo pulled out a flash drive. "I'm paranoid. I keep offline backups of my offline backups. This has copies of the key documents. Not all of them, but enough."

"We need to get those to Elyra," Lina said. "And we need to go dark. No more archive searches. No more digital breadcrumbs."

"Agreed," Jason said. "We've gotten as much as we're going to get through conventional means. Now we work with what we have."

They left separately. Took different routes home. Met at Elyra's building two hours later.

She listened to their report, her expression darkening.

"Malvek's moving," she said finally. "Cleaning house. Making sure the official narrative stays intact."

"Can he do that?" Milo asked. "Just erase records?"

"He has institutional authority. And the classification system is designed to protect state interests. If he convinces the right people that these records threaten stability—" She shrugged. "Then yes. He can erase them. Legally."

"So what do we do?" Jason asked.

"You stop looking for evidence in places he controls. Archives. Digital systems. Official records. Those are all compromised now." She took the flash drive from Milo. "I'll secure this. Offline. Somewhere he can't reach."

"And then?"

"Then you focus on capability. On becoming good enough that evidence doesn't matter. Because when you're competent enough, truth becomes self-evident."

Jason wanted to protest. Wanted to say that evidence mattered, that truth mattered.

But Elyra was right. In a world where powerful people could rewrite history, capability was the only real currency.

"Okay," he said. "We train. We get better. And we stop looking backward."

"Good." Elyra pocketed the flash drive. "Now let's work on your defensive patterns. Because Malvek's next move won't be erasure. It'll be containment. And I want you ready when it comes."

---

That night, Jason lay awake, thinking about everything they'd lost.

Not the files—those were just data. But the proof. The evidence that could have vindicated Elyra. That could have exposed what really happened.

Gone.

Erased as if it had never existed.

*Does it bother you?* RAE asked.

**Yes. Truth should matter.**

*It does. But power matters more, in the short term.*

**That's depressing.**

*It's reality. But reality can be changed. With enough capability. Enough persistence.*

**How long?**

*Unknown. But we're learning. Growing stronger. Someday, we'll be strong enough that Malvek can't simply erase us.*

**And until then?**

*We survive. We train. We become exceptional.*

Jason closed his eyes. Let exhaustion pull him toward sleep.

Tomorrow, they'd resume training. Would work on defensive patterns. Would become a little bit more capable.

And someday—maybe soon, maybe not—they'd be strong enough to stand against institutional power.

But not today.

Today, they were still small. Still learning. Still vulnerable.

And that was okay.

Because every day of training was a day closer to strength.

Each lesson building toward mastery.
