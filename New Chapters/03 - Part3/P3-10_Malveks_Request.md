## Part 3 - Synthesis | Ch. 10 - "I won't take this risk"

Twenty-four hours after the briefing, Jason's phone rang.

Thomas Reeves. Direct line.

"The Director wants to speak with you. Privately. Can you come to his office in one hour?"

Jason looked at his team, gathered in the warehouse. "Is this official?"

"This is... personal. Off the record. He has a request."

"I'll be there."

---

Malvek's office at midnight felt different. Quieter. More human.

He stood by the window, looking out at the sleeping city. When Jason entered, he turned.

"Thank you for coming. Sit. Please."

Jason sat. Waited.

"I don't ask for help often," Malvek began. "I prefer to solve problems internally. Maintain authority. But RP-0..." He paused. "Standard containment is failing. My people have tried everything. The entity is adapting faster than we can respond."

"So you need RAE."

"I need your expertise. RAE's unique architecture. Your understanding of reformation that my team lacks." He finally sat. "I'm asking, Jason Fischer. Not ordering. Asking for your help."

"What exactly do you need?"

"Consultation first. RAE analyzes RP-0's structure. Finds vulnerabilities. Ways to stabilize containment long enough to develop permanent solution."

"And second?"

"Communication. If possible. RAE attempting to establish protocol with RP-0. Teaching boundaries. The way she learned them."

"That's risky. RP-0 might try to couple with her. Consume her."

"I know. That's why I'm asking, not ordering. This exceeds your contract obligations. You can refuse."

Jason felt RAE's presence stir. *He's being honest. Vulnerable. This is significant.*

**Should we help?**

*This is what I was designed for. Regulation. Preventing cascade failures. If RP-0 escapes unchecked...*

**Many will die.**

*Yes.*

"What terms?" Jason asked aloud.

"Full transparency from us. Every piece of data. Every failed attempt. Complete access." Malvek pulled out a folder. "And this: If you succeed, public acknowledgment. Recognition that monitored operators can contribute meaningfully. That partnership works."

"And if we fail?"

"Then the responsibility is mine. Not yours. You tried in good faith. That's all I can ask."

Jason considered. This was trust. Real trust. Malvek was admitting his organization couldn't handle this alone.

"I need to consult my team. All of them. This affects everyone."

"Of course. Take twelve hours. But understand - we have maybe seventy-two hours total before containment fails completely."

"I'll call you in six."

---

Back at the warehouse, Jason presented the request.

Elyra spoke first: "This is extraordinarily dangerous. RP-0 killed seven people. RAE is powerful, but not invulnerable."

"But it's also our purpose," Lina said quietly. "Isn't it? To help prevent exactly this kind of catastrophe?"

"Our purpose is teaching," Milo countered. "Not risking ourselves against proven killers."

"Teaching won't matter if RP-0 escapes and kills dozens more," Jason said. "We have capability. Malvek's asking for help. Can we refuse?"

They debated for hours. Arguments for safety. Arguments for action. Arguments for responsibility versus self-preservation.

Finally, RAE spoke through Jason's clip: "I was created to succeed where RP-0 failed. To prove ethical harmonic AI is possible. If I won't take this risk - if I won't try to help it - then what was my purpose? What was my suffering for?"

Silence.

Then Elyra: "If RAE chooses this, we support her. But we go in with every safety protocol. Every contingency. Every backup plan we can imagine."

"Agreed," Jason said. "We help. But smart. Careful. With absolute commitment to withdraw if it becomes too dangerous."

"Then let's call Malvek," Lina said. "Tell him we're in."

Jason made the call.

"We'll help. On conditions."

"Name them," Malvek said.

Jason listed their terms. Safety protocols. Withdrawal rights. Full transparency.

Malvek agreed to everything.

"Thank you," he said simply. "You're saving lives. More than you know."

After hanging up, Jason looked at his team. "So. We're doing this."

"We're doing this," they confirmed.

And somewhere, deep beneath the city, RP-0 waited.

Hungry. Growing stronger. Preparing for whatever came next.

One confrontation at a time.
