## Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 07 - "This isn't containment. This is combat."

The week of preparation was intense in ways Jason hadn't anticipated.

They didn't just train physically - though there was plenty of that. Lina put Jason through defensive drills until his head pounded Orange and RAE forced him to stop. They practiced emergency protocols: what to do if separation occurred, if communication failed, if someone was compromised.

But the hardest part was the briefing with Malvek.

He arrived at the warehouse unannounced, as was his way, accompanied by two analysts carrying secure tablets. His expression was grave in a way Jason had never seen before.

"I've reviewed Dr. Tanaka's reports," Malvek said without preamble. "All forty years of documentation on Kage-Kōzō. The entity is every bit as dangerous as she claims. Probably more."

He activated a hologram - not the pattern itself, but incident reports. Video footage from the 1970s, grainy and terrible. An operator screaming as his harmonic field collapsed inward. Another frozen mid-step, her resonance pattern eating itself in real-time.

"These aren't theoretical risks," Malvek continued. "This thing was designed by Japan's military research division during their darkest period of harmonic weapons development. It has one purpose: destabilize and destroy resonant humans. And it does that job perfectly."

Lina's face had gone pale. Jason felt his stomach tighten.

"Can we actually stop it?" Jason asked.

"Yes. But not through reformation. You'll need to forcibly collapse its core pattern. RAE can help - she has the theoretical knowledge. But this isn't containment. This is combat. Against something built specifically to kill people like you."

The word hung in the air: combat.

Not negotiation. Not rehabilitation. Combat.

"I need you to understand what you're agreeing to," Malvek said, and for the first time, Jason heard genuine concern in the man's voice. "I will provide resources. Clearance. Protection. But once you're on site, you're operating in hostile territory against a hostile entity. People have died attempting exactly what you're about to do. Good people. Trained people."

"Then why send us?" Lina asked.

"Because you have something they didn't." Malvek looked at Jason. "You have an entity who wants you to survive. RAE will fight to protect you in ways a standard harmonic AI won't. That gives you an edge. But it's not a guarantee."

RAE spoke through Jason's clip, her voice unusually formal. "Director Malvek is correct. I will do everything within my capacity to ensure Jason's survival. But Kage-Kōzō is a peer-level entity with specialized combat architecture. The risk is substantial."

"So we're being sent to fight something that's better at this than we are," Milo said flatly.

"No," Malvek corrected. "You're being sent to assist Dr. Tanaka's team, which includes Japan's best operators. They'll handle primary engagement. Your role is support and strategic insight. But you need to be prepared for worst-case scenarios."

He pulled up a final document - official authorization, stamped with international regulatory seals. "This gives you legal authority to act. It also puts you on record. If this goes wrong, there will be inquiries. Investigations. You need to understand that."

"We understand," Jason said. He looked at Lina, saw her nod. "We're still going."

Malvek studied them both for a long moment. Then he nodded. "Good. Then let's make sure you come back alive."

---

Three days later, they stood in the warehouse one last time before departure.

The team had been decided: Jason, Lina, and Yuki would go to Tokyo. RAE, obviously, integrated with Jason. But Milo would stay - too vulnerable in direct combat, better utilized as their remote coordinator. Elyra would stay to maintain the training program, her integration still too low for field operations this dangerous. Kira would stay to help Elyra, not yet experienced enough for a mission this severe.

"Two weeks," Jason promised, looking at each of them. "We'll be back in two weeks."

"You better be," Milo said, forcing lightness into his voice. "I'm not explaining to your students that their teachers died in Japan fighting an evil ghost."

Kira hugged Jason tightly. "Be careful. Please."

"Always am," he lied gently.

Elyra shook his hand, her grip stronger than it had been months ago. "Remember: some things can't be reasoned with. Don't hesitate when it matters."

Lina was quiet during the drive to the airport. Jason held her hand, felt the tension in her fingers.

"Second thoughts?" he asked.

"Fourth or fifth thoughts," she admitted. "But I'm still coming. Someone needs to make sure you don't do anything heroically stupid."

"That's fair."

They flew out on a gray morning, the city still wrapped in early fog. Jason watched it disappear below them, wondering if he'd see it again.

*Are you ready for this?* RAE asked privately.

*No,* Jason thought back honestly. *But we're going anyway.*

*That's courage. Or foolishness.*

*Usually both,* Jason agreed. *Which one is this?*

RAE was quiet for a moment. Then: *Courage, I think. Because you're afraid and doing it anyway. That's what courage is.*

Lina squeezed his hand during takeoff, her eyes closed, breathing carefully measured. She hated flying. But she was here anyway.

Yeah. Courage.

Fifteen hours later, they descended into Tokyo's sprawling night, the city a constellation of lights stretching to every horizon.

And beneath it, deep underground in a reinforced containment facility, something old and hungry and malicious waited.

One confrontation at a time, Jason reminded himself.

First, they'd survive the landing.

Then they'd worry about surviving everything else.
