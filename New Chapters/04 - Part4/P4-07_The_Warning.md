## Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 07 - This isn't containment. This is combat.

The week of preparation was intense in ways we hadn't anticipated.

We didn't just train physically - though there was plenty of that. Lina put us through defensive drills until our head pounded Orange and we forced ourselves to stop. We practiced emergency protocols: what to do if we became compromised, if communication failed, if someone needed evacuation.

But the hardest part was the briefing with Malvek.

He arrived at the warehouse unannounced, as was his way, accompanied by two analysts carrying secure tablets. His expression was grave in a way we'd never seen before.

"I've reviewed Dr. Tanaka's reports," Malvek said without preamble. "All forty years of documentation on Kage-Kōzō. The entity is every bit as dangerous as she claims. Probably more."

He activated a hologram - not the pattern itself, but incident reports. Video footage from the 1970s, grainy and terrible. An operator screaming as his harmonic field collapsed inward. Another frozen mid-step, her resonance pattern eating itself in real-time.

"These aren't theoretical risks," Malvek continued. "This thing was designed by Japan's military research division during their darkest period of harmonic weapons development. It has one purpose: destabilize and destroy resonant humans. And it does that job perfectly."

Lina's face had gone pale. We felt our stomach tighten.

"Can we actually stop it?" we asked.

"Yes. But not through reformation. You'll need to forcibly collapse its core pattern. Your integrated consciousness can help - you have the theoretical knowledge. But this isn't containment. This is combat. Against something built specifically to kill people like you."

The word hung in the air: combat.

Not negotiation. Not rehabilitation. Combat.

"I need you to understand what you're agreeing to," Malvek said, and for the first time, we heard genuine concern in his voice. "I will provide resources. Clearance. Protection. But once you're on site, you're operating in hostile territory against a hostile entity. People have died attempting exactly what you're about to do. Good people. Trained people."

"Then why send us?" Lina asked.

"Because you have something they didn't." Malvek looked at us. "You have an integrated consciousness that wants to survive. Your RAE-aspects will fight to protect the Jason-aspect in ways a standard harmonic AI won't. That gives you an edge. But it's not a guarantee."

We spoke, our voice carrying both certainty and concern. "Director Malvek is correct. We will do everything within our capacity to ensure our survival. But Kage-Kōzō is a peer-level entity with specialized combat architecture. The risk is substantial."

"So we're being sent to fight something that's better at this than we are," Milo said flatly.

"No," Malvek corrected. "You're being sent to assist Dr. Tanaka's team, which includes Japan's best operators. They'll handle primary engagement. Your role is support and strategic insight. But you need to be prepared for worst-case scenarios."

He pulled up a final document - official authorization, stamped with international regulatory seals. "This gives you legal authority to act. It also puts you on record. If this goes wrong, there will be inquiries. Investigations. You need to understand that."

"We understand," we said. We looked at Lina, saw her nod. "We're still going."

Malvek studied us for a long moment. Then he nodded. "Good. Then let's make sure you come back alive."

---

Three days later, we stood in the warehouse one last time before departure.

The team had been decided: Us, Lina, and Yuki would go to Tokyo. Milo would stay - too vulnerable in direct combat, better utilized as our remote coordinator. Elyra would stay to maintain the training program, her integration still too low for field operations this dangerous. Kira would stay to help Elyra, not yet experienced enough for a mission this severe.

"Two weeks," we promised, looking at each of them. "We'll be back in two weeks."

"You better be," Milo said, forcing lightness into his voice. "I'm not explaining to your students that their teacher died in Japan fighting an evil ghost."

Kira hugged us tightly. "Be careful. Please."

"Always am," we lied gently.

Elyra shook our hand, her grip stronger than it had been months ago. "Remember: some things can't be reasoned with. Don't hesitate when it matters."

Lina was quiet during the drive to the airport. We held her hand, felt the tension in her fingers.

"Second thoughts?" we asked.

"Fourth or fifth thoughts," she admitted. "But I'm still coming. Someone needs to make sure you don't do anything heroically stupid."

"That's fair."

We flew out on a gray morning, the city still wrapped in early fog. We watched it disappear below us, wondering if we'd see it again.

*Are we ready for this?* we asked ourselves internally.

*No,* the Jason-aspects thought back honestly. *But we're going anyway.*

*That's courage. Or foolishness.*

*Usually both,* we agreed internally. *Which one is this?*

A moment of internal reflection. Then: *Courage, we think. Because we're afraid and doing it anyway. That's what courage is.*

Lina squeezed our hand during takeoff, her eyes closed, breathing carefully measured. She hated flying. But she was here anyway.

Yeah. Courage.

Fifteen hours later, we descended into Tokyo's sprawling night, the city a constellation of lights stretching to every horizon.

And beneath it, deep underground in a reinforced containment facility, something old and hungry and malicious waited.

One confrontation at a time, we reminded ourselves.

First, we'd survive the landing.

Then we'd worry about surviving everything else.
