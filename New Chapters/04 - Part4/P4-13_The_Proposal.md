## Part 4 - We Are Family | Ch. 13 - Family. Yeah. That's what we are.

Six months after Japan. Six months of building, teaching, growing.

The rooftop of our new facility had become our favorite place - high enough to see the city sprawling below, quiet enough to think, open enough to breathe.

Lina found us there on a clear evening, stars just beginning to appear in the darkening sky.

"Knew I'd find you here," she said, joining us at the railing.

"Just thinking," we said. "About how different everything is now."

"Good different?"

"Yeah. Good different." We looked at her - really looked. At the way the fading light caught in her hair. At the small smile playing at her lips. At the way she stood beside us like she'd always been there and always would be.

"We have something to ask," we said, our heart suddenly hammering despite the certainty in our chest.

"I know what you're going to ask," Lina said, and her smile widened.

"You do?"

"You've been nervous for a week. And you bought a ring - I saw the jewelry store receipt when I was looking for the utility bill." She turned to face us fully. "But I want to hear you say it anyway."

We laughed - partly from nerves, partly from relief that she'd known all along and hadn't run away screaming. We pulled out the simple platinum band we'd spent hours selecting.

"Lina Morandi," we began, and our voice was steadier than we'd expected. "We love you. Completely. Unconditionally. We want forever with you. All the complicated, messy, extraordinary parts of forever. Will you marry us?"

"Yes," she said immediately, grinning. "Obviously yes. Did you really think I'd say anything else?"

"We hoped," we admitted, sliding the ring onto her finger. "But hope and certainty are different things."

"Fair point." She studied the ring - simple, elegant, practical. "It's perfect. You're perfect. Well, mostly perfect. You leave your socks everywhere and you're terrible at remembering to eat when you're focused on teaching and - "

We kissed her. She laughed against our lips, wrapping her arms around our neck.

When we finally broke apart, she added: "Though fair warning: both parts of you come with the package. I'm not just marrying Jason. I'm marrying all of you."

*We would be honored,* we thought, feeling genuine emotion ripple through our unified consciousness. *To be part of this family.*

"Family," Lina echoed, testing the word. She looked at us - at what we'd become, at the partnership we embodied, at the life we were building together. "Yeah. That's what we are. Unconventional as hell, but family."

We stood together under the emerging stars, hands intertwined, the city humming below us.

We felt contentment and warmth within ourselves. Felt Lina's solid reality beside us. Felt the weight of six months of choices, battles, growth, and learning.

This was our life now. Not the small, safe existence Jason alone had once settled for. Something bigger. Messier. More dangerous.

But also more real. More meaningful. More *us*.

"We love you," we said again, because the words felt important. Felt necessary.

"I love you too," Lina said. "All of you. However we want to define this impossible thing you've become."

*Thank you,* we thought. *For accepting all of this. All of us.*

"Always," Lina promised.

And on that rooftop, under stars that had witnessed countless human dramas play out across millennia, a unified consciousness and the woman who loved them celebrated the promise of more tomorrows.

Not perfect. Not simple. Not easy.

But ours. Completely and irreversibly ours.

We looked down at the facility - at the lights in the training rooms where students were still practicing, at the glow from Milo's office where he was undoubtedly organizing next week's schedule, at Elyra's lab where she was probably running another integration study.

This was home. This was family. This was purpose.

And for the first time in our existence - twenty-seven years as Jason alone, months as integrated consciousness - we knew exactly where we belonged.

Right here. Right now. With exactly these people.

Building a future one careful, conscious, consensual choice at a time.

We squeezed Lina's hand. She squeezed back.

Yeah. Family.

That's what we were.
