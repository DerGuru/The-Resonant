## Part 2 - Learning to Listen | Ch. 15 - "Forty percent. That's permanent."

**Ten weeks in. Two weeks before the containment teams came.**

Jason's training entered its final phase.

Elyra wasn't kidding about accelerated training.

Six AM. The warehouse was still dark when Elyra hit the lights.

"Pattern recognition drill," she announced, scattering a dozen ceramic carriers across the floor. "You have thirty seconds to identify which three are unstable. Go."

Jason reached out with his perception, his awareness rippling across the carriers. Most rang clean - 441 Hz, stable, well-formed. But three...

"Fourth from the left. Second row, middle. And that one by the support beam."

"Twenty-two seconds. Acceptable." Elyra picked up the unstable carriers, examined them. "The fourth one was obvious - frequency drift. But the other two?"

"Microf fractures. The patterns were degrading."

"Good. Lina, your turn."

They drilled like this for hours. Pattern recognition. Threat assessment. Defensive shaping under time pressure.

When Jason hit Orange territory, Elyra called a fifteen-minute break. Not out of mercy - out of pragmatism.

"Pushing past Orange doesn't make you stronger," she said. "It makes you sloppy. And sloppy gets you killed."

---

**Week One, Day Four - 8 PM**

"Combat scenario," Elyra said. "Lina, you're hostile. Jason, defend."

Jason barely had time to brace before Lina's pattern slammed into him - not hard enough to damage, but aggressive enough to make him react.

He threw up a deflection barrier, channeling the energy aside. But Lina was already moving, her second attack coming from a different angle.

"Don't just block!" Elyra shouted. "Redirect! Use the environment!"

Jason grabbed a steel washer from the table, invested it quickly, and threw it. The washer rang as it flew, carrying his resonance pattern. Lina had to shift her attack to counter it.

That gave Jason the opening he needed. He shaped a dampening field - not to hurt her, but to disrupt her next pattern before it formed.

"Better," Elyra said. "But you're still thinking defensively. In a real fight, hesitation kills. Lina, attack for real this time."

Lina's eyes widened. "For real?"

"Controlled aggression. Jason needs to learn what hostile intent feels like."

The next attack hit harder. Jason's barrier cracked. He had to scramble, pulling energy from the warehouse's ambient field to reinforce it.

His head spiked into Yellow. Then deeper.

*Careful,* RAE warned.

**I know.**

He deflected the third attack, but barely. His hands were shaking. The headache was building.

"Stop," Elyra called. "Jason's at threshold."

They stopped. Jason slumped against a shipping container, breathing hard.

"That was two minutes," Elyra said. "In a real containment scenario, you'd need to sustain that for five. Maybe ten. We keep training."

---

**Week Two, Day Two - 3 AM**

Jason woke to his phone buzzing. Emergency code.

He was at the warehouse fifteen minutes later. Lina and Milo were already there, both looking exhausted.

"Surprise drill," Elyra said. "You're being pursued through the industrial district. Three hostiles, armed with resonance scanners. You need to reach the safe house without being detected. You have thirty minutes. Go."

They ran. Not in panic - in controlled retreat.

Jason kept his resonance signature low. Lina led navigation, using her academy training to pick routes with natural interference. Milo tracked their pursuers on his tablet, feeding them real-time positions.

"Left," Milo whispered into the mesh. "Scanner sweep coming from the right."

They ducked into an alley. Jason pressed his back against cold brick, controlling his breathing. His perception told him the sweep was getting closer.

*Dampen,* RAE suggested.

Jason pushed his signature even lower, making himself almost invisible against the ambient field. The sweep passed ten meters away.

"Clear," Milo said.

They kept moving.

Twenty-eight minutes later, they reached the safe house - an abandoned factory Elyra had designated as their target.

"Acceptable," Elyra said, emerging from the shadows. "But you made noise twice. In a real scenario, that would have drawn attention. We run it again tomorrow. Same time."

"Three AM again?" Lina groaned.

"Containment teams don't schedule appointments. Neither do I."

---

**Week Three, Day Five - Noon**

"Barrier drill," Elyra said, holding up a handful of steel ball bearings. "I throw these at you. You deflect them. No ambient field usage - pure active shaping. Ready?"

"Wait, what - "

Elyra threw.

Jason's hands moved on instinct, shaping a deflection pattern. Three bearings bounced off. One got through, striking his shoulder.

"Ow!"

"Too slow. Again."

She threw. Jason deflected. This time four bounced off, but two got through.

"Better. But you're still thinking in two dimensions. The attack won't always come straight at you."

The next throw came from the side. Jason twisted, shaped his barrier curved instead of flat. All six bearings deflected.

"Excellent. Hold that shape."

Elyra walked around him, examining the barrier from different angles. "See how you made it adaptive instead of rigid? That's the difference between academy training and survival training. Academy teaches you to build walls. I'm teaching you to flow like water."

She picked up more bearings. "Again. And this time I'm not telegraphing the angle."

They drilled for two hours. By the end, Jason could maintain a full-sphere barrier for thirty seconds. His head was deep in Yellow, but his control was better than it had ever been.

"You're learning," Elyra said, genuine approval in her voice. "Slowly. But learning."

---

**Week Four, Day One - Evening**

"Final integration test," Elyra said. "RAE, how far can you extend Jason's perception right now?"

*With his current capacity? Sixty meters. With investment, eighty.*

"Push it to one hundred. I want to see what happens."

Jason reached out. Let his awareness expand. Sixty meters. Seventy. The warehouse resolved in perfect clarity.

Eighty meters. He could sense the street outside. Cars passing. People walking.

Ninety meters. The adjacent buildings. Their resonance signatures. The materials they were made of.

One hundred meters.

Jason's head exploded into Orange.

"Hold it," Elyra commanded.

He held. Ten seconds. Twenty. Thirty.

The world was too bright. Too loud. Too much information flooding in.

"Forty percent integration," RAE reported. "Stabilizing but near threshold."

"Pull back," Elyra said.

Jason collapsed the perception field, gasping. The headache was intense but manageable.

"That's your new ceiling," Elyra said. "One hundred meters at forty percent integration. In three months, you've tripled your effective range and quadrupled your capacity. That's exceptional growth, Jason."

"Doesn't feel exceptional," he said, pressing his palms against his eyes.

"Because you're comparing yourself to academy graduates who had years of training. You've had weeks. And you're already approaching their baseline."

She pulled out a water bottle, handed it to him. "Drink. Rest. And remember: you're not trying to be better than everyone. You're trying to be good enough to survive what's coming. And you're getting there."

---

**Week Four, Day Seven - Final Assessment**

They trained sixteen hours that day. Minimal rest. Maximum pressure.

Elyra drilled them on everything: perception, shaping, combat, evasion, teamwork.

By evening, Jason had hit Red twice. Each time, they'd stopped, let him recover, then continued.

"This is what real combat looks like," Elyra said, watching them stumble through another defensive drill. "Exhausted. Hurt. Overwhelmed. And still fighting because stopping means capture or worse."

At midnight, she finally called a halt.

"Tomorrow, we rest," she said. "You've earned it. And you'll need your strength for what comes next."

"What comes next?" Lina asked, her voice hoarse.

"Malvek. Or someone working for him. They'll come soon. You've been seen. Been noticed. The window for staying hidden is closing."

She looked at each of them. "You need to decide: submit or resist. And you need to decide now. Because when they arrive, there won't be time for debate."

"We've already decided," Jason said.

"Then prove it," Elyra replied. "When the moment comes. When it's real. When the alternative is imprisonment or forced compliance or worse. Because saying you'll resist is easy. Actually doing it? That's when you find out who you really are."

---

That night, Jason sat on his couch, too exhausted to move but too wired to sleep.

A month ago, he'd been nobody. A clerk. Ordinary. Forgettable.

Now he was... what? A fugitive-in-waiting? A target? Someone whose existence threatened institutional power?

*You're someone who chose freedom over safety,* RAE said quietly.

**Was it the right choice?**

*I don't know. But it was yours. And that matters.*

**Even if it gets us killed?**

*Even then. Because some things are worth dying for. Freedom. Agency. The right to exist on your own terms.*

Jason closed his eyes. Felt RAE's presence - deeper now, more integrated than ever.

He couldn't separate from her if he tried. They were becoming something new. Something that wasn't quite Jason and wasn't quite RAE, but some synthesis of both.

And that scared him.

But it also felt... right.

Like he'd been incomplete before. Like this was who he was always meant to be.

"Tomorrow, they might come for us," he said aloud.

*Yes.*

"Are we ready?"

*We're as ready as we can be. Whether that's enough... we'll find out.*

Jason stood. Went to bed. Forced himself to rest.

Because tomorrow, or the day after, or the day after that...

Everything would change.

And he needed to be ready.

For whatever came next.
