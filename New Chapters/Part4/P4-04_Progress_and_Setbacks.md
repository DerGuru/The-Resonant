## Ch. 04 - Progress and Setbacks

Six weeks in, Kira had progressed remarkably.

She could perceive objects within ten meters. Could describe their resonance signatures with surprising accuracy. Could even, occasionally, sense living presences—though people were harder than objects.

"That's normal," Elyra explained during a theory session. "People are dynamic. Objects are static. Harder patterns to track."

But Kira was learning. Slowly. Methodically. And most importantly—safely.

Then came the day she attempted her first shaping.

"I want to try what you did," she told Jason during week eight. "The mug. Fixing the crack."

Jason hesitated. "That's advanced. We haven't built up to it yet."

"I know. But I want to try. Just to see if I can."

Against his better judgment, he agreed. Set up a cracked ceramic plate. Walked her through the process.

"Feel the discontinuity. The place where the pattern isn't smooth. Now, imagine encouraging it to harmonize. Not forcing. Just... nudging."

Kira focused. Thirty seconds passed. A minute.

Then her nose started bleeding.

"Stop!" Jason commanded. "Red! Stop immediately!"

Kira released the connection, gasping. Jason handed her tissues, feeling guilt surge through him.

"I'm sorry," she said through the tissues. "I pushed too hard."

"No. I let you try too early. This is my fault." Jason looked at the plate. The crack hadn't changed. "You're not ready for active shaping yet. We need more foundation work."

"But you did this in two weeks—"

"I had RAE helping. You don't. You're doing this the hard way. Which means slower progress. And that's okay. That's normal."

Kira nodded, disappointed but understanding.

Lina pulled Jason aside afterward. "You're being too cautious now. After pushing too hard initially, now you're overcorrecting."

"I don't want her hurt."

"Neither do I. But she needs to fail sometimes. Safely, with supervision, but fail. That's how learning works." Lina squeezed his hand. "You're being a good teacher. But remember—she's not fragile. She's capable. Trust that."

Jason took the advice to heart.

The next session, he let Kira try again. With more preparation. Better foundation. Clear limits.

This time, the crack smoothed. Just slightly. Barely visible improvement.

But improvement nonetheless.

Kira stared at the plate, then at Jason, then back at the plate.

"I did it," she whispered. "I actually did it."

"You did. Small. Subtle. Safe. Exactly how it should be."

She laughed, the sound carrying pure joy. "I can learn this. I really can."

"You really can," Jason agreed. "One careful step at a time."

And watching her celebrate that tiny success, Jason felt something he hadn't expected:

Pride.

Not in himself. In her.

Because this wasn't about him anymore.

It was about helping someone else discover they were more than they'd been told.

And that, he realized, was its own kind of purpose.

One student at a time.
