# Ch. 02 - "You're not me. You need your own path."

### The Interview

Kira Chen arrived exactly on time.

Clean but worn clothes - jeans, jacket, work boots. Hair in practical ponytail. Hands showing warehouse calluses. When she saw the training space, her eyes went wide.

"This is... wow."

"Welcome," Jason said, extending his hand. "I'm Jason. This is Lina, Milo, and Elyra."

"I know." Kira shook hands, grip firm but nervous. "I've read about you. Online. The forums. You're the one who coupled with a harmonic entity. Who made it work."

"RAE," Jason confirmed. "Yes. She's here now, actually. Part of me."

"That must be amazing? Terrifying?"

"Both. Depending on the day." Jason gestured to a chair. "But we're here to talk about you. Tell us your story."

---

Kira's story was honest.

Tested at sixteen. Scored minimal potential. Told she'd never be more than baseline. Accepted it. Got warehouse job. Lived normal life.

Except she kept feeling things. Resonance signatures she shouldn't notice. Patterns in harmonics. Small things. Subtle things. But persistent.

"I thought I was imagining it," she said. "But then I found the forums. Read about people like you. And I realized - maybe I wasn't tested wrong. Maybe I just needed different training."

"What makes you think you can learn?" Elyra asked directly.

"Because I have to. Because if I don't try, I'll spend my whole life wondering 'what if.' And I can't live with that."

Jason knew that feeling intimately.

They spent an hour questioning her. Ethics. Limits. What she'd do with capability if she gained it. Every answer thoughtful, careful, honest.

"We'll take you," Jason said finally. "As our first student. But understand - this is experimental. We're learning to teach while teaching you. There will be mistakes."

"I understand," Kira said, eyes wet. "Thank you. You have no idea what this means."

"I do, actually," Jason said quietly. "I was you. Not long ago. Terrified and hopeful and desperate to prove I was more than my test results."

Training would start Monday. Three days a week. Two hours per session. Slow. Safe. Foundation before danger.

After she left, Lina said, "She reminds me of someone."

"Yeah," Jason admitted. "Me too."

---

### Week One: Teaching is Harder

Jason thought teaching would be easy. He'd learned these techniques. Could do them instinctively now. Surely explaining them would be simple?

It wasn't.

"Feel the resonance," he told Kira on day three. "Just... open your awareness and feel it."

"I'm trying." Frustration evident in her voice. "I don't understand what I'm supposed to feel."

"It's like peripheral vision, but for harmonics."

"That doesn't help."

Ten minutes passed. Twenty. Kira grew more frustrated. Jason grew impatient. Why wasn't this working? He'd learned this in days. Why was she struggling?

*Because she's not you,* RAE reminded gently. *She doesn't have me helping her perceive. She doesn't have your natural permeability. She's learning the hard way. The normal way.*

**But I thought - **

*You thought teaching would be doing in reverse. It's not. It's understanding someone else's learning style. Which you haven't learned yet.*

Jason took a breath. "Let's take a break."

"I'm sorry," Kira said, tears of frustration welling. "I'm trying really hard."

"I know. And the fault isn't yours. It's mine. I'm teaching you the way I learned. But you're not me. You need your own path."

Lina, who'd been watching from the side, approached. "May I try?"

Jason nodded, stepping back, feeling equal parts relief and chagrin.

Lina sat across from Kira. "Forget perception for now. Let's start simpler. Touch the bowl. Actually touch it with your hands."

Kira did.

"Good. Now, keeping your eyes closed, describe what you feel. Texture. Temperature. Weight."

"It's... smooth. Cool. Light."

"Excellent. Now, still touching it, imagine the bowl is speaking to you. Not words - just... presence. It's saying 'I am here. I am ceramic. I am stable.' Can you feel that?"

Long silence. Then, tentatively: "Maybe? It's really faint, but... yes? I think I feel it?"

"That's perception. That tiny awareness. That's the beginning." Lina smiled. "You're doing it. You just didn't recognize it because it's subtle. And subtle is correct."

Kira opened her eyes, amazement on her face. "That was it? That tiny thing?"

"Perception isn't dramatic. It's noticing what's already there."

Jason watched, learning his own lesson. Lina had succeeded where he'd failed because she'd met Kira where she was, not where Jason had been.

*Lesson learned,* RAE observed. *Teaching requires empathy, not just knowledge.*

---

### Weeks Two Through Eight: Progress

Week by week, Kira improved.

By week four, she could perceive objects within ten meters. Could describe their resonance signatures with surprising accuracy. Still struggled with living presences - people were harder than objects - but that was normal.

"People are dynamic," Elyra explained during theory. "Objects are static. Harder patterns to track."

Jason adjusted his teaching. Watched Lina work with Kira. Learned patience. Learned to celebrate tiny victories. Learned that progress looked different for everyone.

By week eight, Kira asked: "Can I try shaping? Like you did with the mug?"

Jason hesitated. "That's advanced. We haven't built up to it yet."

"I know. But I want to try. Just to see if I can."

Against his better judgment - or maybe because he remembered his own eagerness - he agreed. Set up a cracked ceramic plate. Walked her through the process.

"Feel the discontinuity. The place where the pattern isn't smooth. Now, imagine encouraging it to harmonize. Not forcing. Just... nudging."

Kira focused. Thirty seconds passed. A minute.

Then her nose started bleeding.

"Stop!" Jason commanded. "Red! Stop immediately!"

Kira released the connection, gasping. Jason handed her tissues, guilt surging through him.

"I'm sorry," she said through the tissues. "I pushed too hard."

"No. I let you try too early. My fault." He examined the plate. The crack hadn't changed. "You're not ready for active shaping yet. We need more foundation work."

"But you did this in two weeks - "

"I had RAE helping. You don't. You're doing this the hard way. Which means slower progress. And that's okay. That's normal. That's *safe*."

Kira nodded, disappointed but understanding.

Lina pulled Jason aside afterward. "You're being too cautious now. After pushing too hard initially, you're overcorrecting."

"I don't want her hurt."

"Neither do I. But she needs to fail sometimes. Safely, with supervision, but fail. That's how learning works." Lina squeezed his hand. "She's not fragile. She's capable. Trust that."

---

### Week Twelve: Success

Three more weeks of practice. More foundation. Better preparation. Clear limits.

Then, one afternoon, Kira attempted the cracked plate again.

This time, with proper groundwork: The crack smoothed. Just slightly. Barely visible improvement.

But improvement nonetheless.

Kira stared at the plate. Then at Jason. Then back at the plate.

"I did it," she whispered. "I actually did it."

"You did. Small. Subtle. Safe. Exactly how it should be." Jason felt pride swell in his chest. "That's the hardest part - learning to celebrate the tiny victories. Because that's what this path is. Tiny, careful changes that add up to something meaningful."

She laughed, the sound carrying pure joy. "I can learn this. I really can."

"You really can," Jason agreed. "One careful step at a time."

Watching her celebrate that tiny success, Jason felt something unexpected:

Pride. Not in himself. In *her*.

Because this wasn't about him anymore. Wasn't about proving his own capability or vindicating his own journey.

It was about helping someone else discover they were more than they'd been told.

And that, he realized, was its own kind of power.

The kind that mattered most.

One student at a time.
