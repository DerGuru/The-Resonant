## Ch. 03 - Teaching is Harder

Week one was humbling.

"Feel the resonance," Jason told Kira. "Just... open your awareness and feel it."

"I'm trying. I don't understand what I'm supposed to feel."

"It's like peripheral vision, but for harmonics."

"That doesn't help."

Ten minutes passed. Twenty. Kira grew frustrated. Jason grew impatient.

*She's not you,* RAE reminded. *She doesn't have me helping. She needs her own path.*

Lina intervened. Sat across from Kira. "Touch the bowl. Actually touch it. Describe texture, temperature, weight."

Kira did. "Smooth. Cool. Light."

"Now imagine it's speaking to you. Not words. Just presence. 'I am here. I am ceramic. I am stable.'"

Long pause. Then: "Maybe? Really faint, but... yes?"

"That's perception. That tiny awareness. That's the beginning."

Kira's eyes lit. "That was it? That tiny thing?"

"Perception isn't dramatic. It's noticing what's already there."

Jason watched, feeling equal parts relief and chagrin. Lina had succeeded where he'd failed. Because she'd met Kira where she was, not where Jason had been.

*Lesson learned,* RAE observed. *Teaching requires empathy, not just knowledge.*

Week by week, Kira improved. Slowly. Safely. With mistakes and corrections and patient guidance.

By week eight, she attempted her first shaping. Pushed too hard. Nosebleed. Red status.

"I'm sorry," she said.

"No. I let you try too early. My fault." Jason adjusted their approach. More foundation. Less rush.

Next attempt, with better preparation: The crack smoothed. Slightly. Barely visible.

But improvement.

Kira stared, then laughed with pure joy. "I did it. I actually did it."

"You did. Small. Subtle. Safe. Exactly how it should be."

Watching her celebrate that tiny success, Jason felt pride. Not in himself. In her.

This wasn't about him anymore.

It was about helping someone else discover they were more than they'd been told.

One student at a time.
