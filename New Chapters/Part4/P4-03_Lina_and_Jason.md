# Ch. 03 - "RAE comes with the package"

Date nights were rare.

Between training Kira, coordinating with Malvek's organization, and maintaining their own practice, Jason and Lina had maybe one evening a week that belonged just to them.

Tonight was one of those evenings.

They'd chosen something simple: dinner at a quiet restaurant neither of them had been to before. No resonance discussions. No student progress reports. Just... being together.

"Your father asked about you," Lina said over appetizers. "Wanted to know if you're being safe."

"What did you tell him?"

"That you're as safe as anyone working with resonance can be. Which worried him, but he accepted it." She smiled. "He also asked when we're getting married."

Jason nearly choked on his wine. "He what?"

"Relax. I told him we're taking things slow. That we're figuring it out as we go." She reached across the table, took his hand. "But I've been thinking about it. What this is. What we are."

"And?"

"And I'm happy. Really happy. For the first time in years." She squeezed his hand. "You make me better. Challenge me. Support me. And I think I do the same for you."

"You do. Absolutely."

"So... where do you see this going? Long term?"

Jason considered that. "Honestly? I see us together. Building this program. Teaching people. Maybe someday having a life that's more than just work. Kids, even. If that's something you want."

"It is. Someday. Not yet, but someday." Lina paused. "But there's something we need to talk about. RAE."

Jason's stomach tightened. "What about her?"

"I need to know: When we're together—really together, intimately—is she... there? Listening? Aware?"

*This is important,* RAE said quietly. *Answer honestly.*

"She can be," Jason said carefully. "But she's not. She gives us privacy. Complete privacy. She... steps back. Goes quiet. Lets those moments be just ours."

"Really?"

"Really." He looked at Lina seriously. "RAE understands boundaries. Respects them. She knows there are parts of my life that should be just mine. Or just ours. And she honors that."

Lina was quiet for a moment. Then: "Can I ask her directly?"

Jason nodded. *RAE? Can you project? Just for a moment?*

A nearby water glass hummed softly. "Lina Morandi. I give you and Jason complete privacy during intimate moments. I am not always listening. Not always present. Some experiences should be yours alone. I respect that."

"Thank you," Lina said. "I needed to hear that. Not because I don't trust you, but because I needed to understand the boundaries."

"Understood. And appreciated." The glass fell silent.

Lina looked at Jason. "Okay. I'm good. Really good."

---

Later, back at Jason's apartment, they stood on the balcony under stars.

"I have a question," Lina said. "Completely hypothetical. If we had kids someday... would RAE affect them? Would they be born... different?"

"I don't know," Jason admitted. "RAE's integrated with me, but genetics are complex. It's possible they'd have higher natural permeability. Or maybe nothing. We honestly don't know."

"Would that scare you? Having kids who might be like you?"

"A little. But also... no? Because we'd know how to help them. How to teach them safely. And they'd have Elyra. And the whole program we're building." He turned to face her. "Would it scare you?"

"Maybe. But I'd rather have kids with you—whatever that means—than not have kids at all."

They kissed. Slowly. Deeply. A promise of future possibilities.

When they pulled apart, Jason said, "I love you. Completely. And I want whatever future we can build together."

"Me too," Lina whispered. "Me too."

---

Inside, later, in the quiet darkness, they made love with the certainty of two people who'd chosen each other deliberately. Not perfect. Not without complications.

But real. And right. And theirs.

And RAE, true to her word, was silent.

Giving them privacy. Giving them space.

Because some moments belonged only to the two of them.

And that respect, more than anything, made Jason certain:

This could work.

All of it.

The teaching. The relationship. The future.

One careful choice at a time.
