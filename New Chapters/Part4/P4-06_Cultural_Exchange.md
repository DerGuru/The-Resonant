# Ch. 06 - "Or because I'm broken enough to say yes"

They debated for hours after Yuki left.

The warehouse felt different in the late evening—shadows longer, the hum of equipment more pronounced. Jason paced while the others sat around the table, each processing what they'd just heard.

"We reformed RP-0," Jason said, breaking the silence. "It was hostile at first. Dangerous. But we found a way. Maybe we can—"

"No," Elyra said quietly. Her tone wasn't harsh, just certain. "This is different, Jason."

"How do you know?"

"Because I've seen entities like this before. Before the ritual. Before everything fell apart." She tapped the tablet Yuki had left behind, pulling up the Kage-Kōzō's pattern again. "Look at the core architecture. See how the harmonics fold back on themselves? That's not damage. That's design. It's meant to corrupt other resonance fields on contact."

Lina leaned in, studying the pattern. Her academy training made her fluent in reading these diagrams in ways Jason still struggled with. "She's right. RP-0 was chaotic but not intrinsically malicious. This..." She traced a line through the hologram. "This is predatory. Built to consume resonant humans and grow stronger from their patterns."

"How many has it killed?" Milo asked, his voice tight.

Yuki had left them her files—complete access, nothing hidden. Jason pulled up the casualty reports. Started reading. Stopped after the third one.

"Twelve," he said quietly. "That they know of. Before they got it contained in 1978."

The reports were clinical in their language, but the horror bled through anyway. Operators found drained. Resonance patterns collapsed. Some had lingered for days before dying, their harmonic fields eating themselves from within.

"Jesus," Milo whispered.

RAE spoke through Jason's whisper clip, her voice measured but grave. "I've been analyzing the data Dr. Tanaka provided. Cross-referencing with my fragmentary memories of similar research. Some entities cannot be redeemed. I was built with ethical constraints—incomplete, but foundational. RP-0 lacked them entirely but possessed the flexibility to learn, to adapt, to choose cooperation. But an entity built specifically for harm, with that harm as its core architecture..." She paused, and Jason could feel her genuine uncertainty. "That requires a different response."

"You're saying we should destroy it," Lina said.

"I'm saying reformation may not be possible. And attempting it may cost lives we cannot afford to lose."

Kira had been quiet until now, sitting cross-legged on the floor, her youth making the weight of the conversation sit uneasily on her shoulders. "Is that what we do now? Decide which entities get to exist?"

"No," Jason said immediately. "We don't decide alone. But if Yuki's right, if this thing will kill hundreds when it breaks containment... then we have a responsibility to act."

"Responsibility to who?" Kira pressed. "The people it might hurt? Or the entity itself?"

It was a fair question. An important one.

Elyra answered, her voice gentle but firm. "Both. If there was a way to help it become something else, we would. But you can't teach a weapon to be a tool when weaponry is all it knows. Some things are too broken to fix. Or were never whole to begin with."

The room fell silent again.

Finally, Lina spoke. "If we go—and I'm not saying we should—we don't go blind. We see it ourselves. We verify everything Yuki told us. We make absolutely certain there's no other option."

"Agreed," Jason said.

Milo pulled out his notebook—the same one he'd used to document their safety protocols, now filling with contingencies for international operations. "We'd need Malvek's approval. Probably official clearance. This isn't a local operation."

"Yuki said she had his approval already," Jason remembered. "But yeah, we should confirm. And... we can't all go. Someone needs to stay here. Keep the program running. Keep the students safe."

"I'll stay," Elyra said immediately. "My integration is too low for field operations anyway. And the students trust me."

"Kira nodded. "I'll stay too. Help Elyra."

That left Jason, Lina, and Milo for the Tokyo operation. Plus Yuki and whatever team she had waiting.

Jason looked at Lina. "You don't have to come. This is dangerous. More dangerous than anything we've done."

She met his eyes, and her expression was complicated—part resignation, part determination, part something that might have been dark humor. "I'm coming because I don't trust you to survive without me. Or because I'm competent enough to help. Or because I'm broken enough to say yes to things like this." Her hand found his. "Pick whichever reason makes you feel better."

Despite everything, Jason smiled. "All three?"

"All three works."

Milo cleared his throat. "So we're doing this. Going to Japan. Potentially destroying a hostile entity. Cool. Cool cool cool. Not terrifying at all."

"One week," Jason said, decision made. "We train. We prepare. We verify everything with Malvek. And then we help Dr. Tanaka do what needs to be done."

*Are you certain?* RAE asked privately.

*No,* Jason thought back. *But that's never stopped us before.*

*True. And Jason?*

*Yeah?*

*Thank you for not choosing this lightly. For questioning. That's what makes you worth preserving.*

Jason felt warmth spread through his chest—RAE's equivalent of a reassuring touch.

They would go to Tokyo. They would face something built to destroy.

And they would do it together.

One week to prepare for something none of them truly understood.

It would have to be enough.
