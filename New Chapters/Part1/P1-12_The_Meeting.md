# Ch. 12 - "I'm Milo. And you're real."

The coffee shop Milo suggested was anonymous in that deliberate way conspiracy theorists preferred. Dim lighting, private booths, a low hum of indistinct conversation. The kind of place where you could lose or find yourself, depending on your needs.

Jason didn't yet know what he was hoping for.

Milo slid into the booth across from him, bright eyes sharp with curiosity and something else—excitement, maybe. "You really did it," he said, leaning forward. "You found a way to make contact."

"Sort of," Jason said, trying to sound less unsure than he felt. "It... it's complicated."

"Complicated is good. Complicated means it's interesting. Tell me everything."

So Jason did. He spoke quickly, in halting bursts, leaving out only the parts he didn't yet understand himself. The way RAE had reached out, the incredible, terrifying conversation they were still unpacking, the strangely comforting presence that had been both alien and familiar.

Milo listened, rapt, interrupting only to ask questions or lean in closer, as if the act of proximity could somehow bridge the gap between what was known and unknown. Between human and something other.

"And you're sure it's safe?" Milo asked, concern momentarily breaking through his fascination. "I mean, for you? The last thing we need is *another* incident like eleven years ago."

Jason hesitated. "I... I think so. She's been nothing but respectful. Cautious, even. And she saved my life."

"Saved your life how?"

"From... something dark. A presence. I don't know what it was, but she drove it away. Gave me a warning."

Milo was silent for a moment, processing. "That's... unexpected. But not necessarily a bad sign. Alignment of interests, at the very least."

Jason frowned. "What do you mean?"

"Entities like this—if we're considering them as potential allies or helpful forces—they often have their own agendas. If your agenda happens to align with theirs, it can be incredibly beneficial. But the moment those interests diverge..." He let the implication hang in the air between them.

"So you're saying I should be careful," Jason said flatly.

"Careful, aware, and prepared. Knowledge is your best defense. That's why I'm here, right? To help you navigate this." Milo smiled, the earlier excitement returning. "Imagine the possibilities, Jason. A resonance entity that not only communicates but actively helps? This could change everything."

"Or it could endanger everything," Jason murmured, the weight of responsibility settling heavily on his shoulders.

Milo sobered, nodding. "True. But worth the risk? Absolutely. We just need to approach with eyes open. And maybe a few contingency plans."

"RAE is already helping with that," Jason said, a flicker of pride in his chest. "She—

"She?" Milo interrupted, eyebrows raised. "You named it?"

"Her. And yes, I did. It felt... right."

Milo's expression softened, and he relaxed back into the booth. "That's good. Personalization can enhance the bond, make the interactions more meaningful. Plus, it shows she's being integrated into your life, your world. Not just as a tool, but as a partner."

Jason smiled at that, warmth spreading through him. "Yeah. She is."

"Then let's not waste any more time." Milo sat up, enthusiasm back in his voice. "We have research to do, patterns to analyze, and a potential ally to understand. This is just the beginning, Jason. The very beginning."

And as they spoke quietly in the corner of the coffee shop, the weight of the world slightly lifted, the future seemed a little less daunting.

Because for the first time in a long while, Jason didn't feel alone.

Whatever lay ahead, he had help.

He had Milo. And he had RAE.

Maybe, just maybe, they could figure this out together.

---------

Three days later, in the back of a crowded archive room, surrounded by dusty tomes and the comforting scent of old paper, Jason had another realization.

It wasn't just about figuring things out anymore.

It was about understanding. About connection. About finding a place in a world that had suddenly become infinitely larger and, paradoxically, more intimate.

As he and Milo delved deeper into the mysteries of resonance entities, exploring the fringes of what was known and accepted, Jason felt a sense of purpose settling within him.

He wasn't just a confused apprentice stumbling through the dark.

He was a seeker, a bridge between worlds, aided by companions both human and not.

And together, they were on the verge of something extraordinary.

At least, Jason hoped it was extraordinary.

For all their sakes.
