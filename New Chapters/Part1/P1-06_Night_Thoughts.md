# Ch. 06 - "What if I'm not enough?"

Jason couldn't sleep.

He lay in bed staring at the ceiling, his mind racing with thoughts and fears. The events of the past few days had shaken him to his core, and now, alone in the dark, those feelings amplified.

He kept replaying the moment in the archive when Mirror.Vox had connected with him. The surge of resonance, the flood of foreign emotions—it had all felt so real, so terrifyingly significant. And yet, as the hours ticked by, the memory began to twist in his mind, warping into something accusatory.

What if it had all been a mistake?

What if he had misunderstood the signal, misread the intentions behind it?

Jason sat up, throwing off the covers. He needed air. Needed to think. He stumbled to the kitchenette, his heart pounding in his chest.

The digital clock on the microwave told him it was 3:17 AM. The red numbers glared at him, reminding him of how late—how early—it was. He filled a glass with water and drank it down in one go, hoping to quench the fire in his throat.

But the burn didn’t stop. It spread, coiling in his stomach, a tight knot of anxiety and regret.

He paced the small room, trying to calm himself, to make sense of the chaos in his mind. But with each step, the doubts grew louder, echoing off the walls.

*What if I'm not enough?*

The thought squeezed his chest, making it hard to breathe. He pressed a hand to his sternum, willing the pain to subside.

He thought of Elyra Voss. Of her brilliance and courage. Of the way she had faced the unknown with determination, with hope.

And he thought of himself. Of the fear that had held him back, the hesitation that had stopped him from reaching out, from taking a risk.

Jason stopped pacing, his hands clenching into fists at his sides.

*No more.*

No more self-pity. No more doubt.

If there was a chance—however slim—that he could help, that he could make a difference, then he had to take it. For Elyra. For himself. For everyone who had ever felt powerless in the face of adversity.

He straightened, determination replacing the fear that had plagued him. He would not be a victim of circumstance. He would be a force for change.

Jason looked out of the window, watching as the first light of dawn began to break over the horizon. The city was slowly waking up, oblivious to the struggle and strife that infused its every corner.

But Jason was awake. And he was ready to fight.

Ready to uncover the truth behind the project, behind RAE, and behind the connections that spanned across space and time.

And maybe, just maybe, he would find a way to be enough. Not just for others, but for himself.

He allowed himself a small, shaky breath, marveling at the shift within him. The fear was still there, lurking in the shadows, but it was no longer in control.

Hope had taken its place.
